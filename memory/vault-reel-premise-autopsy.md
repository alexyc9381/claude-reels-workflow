# VAULT (reel 38) premise autopsy — why a 47s reel averaged 5s

Reel 38 "The Judgment Vault" (hand Fable 5 your past good decisions → it back-solves your hidden decision rules into a file → cheap models read it forever). 47s long, **5s avg watch (~10%)** — the account's worst. Animation quality was NOT the variable (same premium style as ATTACK/CLONE/BLUEPRINT winners). The premise itself was dead on arrival. With a 5s average, ~90% of the diagnosis lives in seconds 0-5 and in what the premise made those seconds EMPTY of.

## The four premise dimensions, scored vs winners

**1. Abstraction hops (action → value): 4-5 hops, terminal value invisible.**
BOOKS = 1 hop (folder of statements → P&L, money). CLONE = 2 hops but hop 2 is priced ("keeps working like Opus for pennies"). VAULT = gather your past good calls → model back-solves invisible rules → file → future cheap models read it → they "decide like you" → unpriced benefit someday. Worse: **the INPUT doesn't exist.** CLONE's input (your Fable setup) and BOOKS' input (a statements folder) already sit on the viewer's machine. VAULT asks for "every good call you've ever made" — a dataset nobody has. Self-identification dies in the first sentence that describes the move.

**2. Pictureability of the artifact: zero.**
A P&L, a stack of specs, a red-ink critique are screenshot-able. "Your judgment as rules" is a text file of aphorisms — the honest output would read like a horoscope ("you favor speed over consensus"). Proof: **the finished reel contains no real screens anywhere** — not an edit oversight, a premise symptom. There was nothing real to show, so the edit was forced into 100% metaphor (cartoon vault crashing from the sky), which is exactly the known 1-2s death pattern from [[reel-retention-hook-teardown]] (no real/concrete value signal on frame 1). This premise also directly violated the already-written **VISCERAL-PAYOFF GATE** in [[reel-winning-formula]]: "a payoff that's an INSIGHT / ANALYSIS / map of your patterns is CEREBRAL and reads BORING" — VAULT is the second violation after WORLDLOAD was flagged, but this one shipped.

**3. Who feels the stake: a triple-filtered sliver, and the stake isn't urgent.**
To care you must (a) track the free-window news, (b) believe your gut decisions are your moat, (c) already run cheap models that read instruction files. ATTACK's stake ("your AI is a yes-man") is felt by every AI user in 1 second. And the loss frame is fake: nobody LOSES their judgment when Fable goes paid — they lose a free back-solve, one abstraction away from any felt pain. No money number, no task with existing demand. It's a meta-premise (optimize how you'll use AI later), while every winner does a first-order task people already want done (books, specs, editing).

**4. Provable on screen: no.**
"It back-solves the private rules you were following" cannot be demonstrated or verified in any shot. Compare: yes-man → harsh-editor is one before/after screenshot; BOOKS' P&L exists or doesn't. The load-bearing claim ("a cheap model can't back-solve judgment this good, only the flagship can") is also pure assertion — it exists only to glue the deadline onto a premise that doesn't need it. Unprovable promise = no proof-shot = nothing to hold anyone past the hook.

## What actually played in seconds 0-5 (the 5s-avg smoking gun)
Cartoon vault crash + "FABLE 5 FREE FOR 3 DAYS" stamp + countdown + VO: "You've got three days left of free Fable 5, and the smartest move ISN'T making something new with it." That's: (1) the same deadline open as reels 36 AND 37 — 3rd consecutive, ~6th overall, fatigued and now filtering instead of hooking; (2) a NEGATION — the first breath tells you what NOT to do; (3) zero concrete noun of value. CLONE won with deadline-line-1 because the very next breath was visceral and concrete ("clone its brain into cheaper models"). VAULT's next concrete noun arrives ~20s in and it's "a file."

## Root causes, weighted by how much they explain the 5s avg
1. (9/10) Seconds 0-5 carry zero concrete value signal: recycled deadline + negation hook, no real screen, no artifact noun.
2. (8/10) The premise has no pictureable, provable artifact — a cerebral insight-file payoff (violated the standing visceral-payoff gate); this is the upstream cause that made #1 unavoidable.
3. (6/10) Deadline-lever fatigue + fake urgency: 3rd consecutive open on the same lever, and the premise works without the deadline (the tie-in is an unverifiable assertion).
4. (6/10) Abstraction distance: 4-5 hops, input dataset the viewer doesn't possess, terminal value unpriced.
5. (5/10) Narrow, non-urgent stake: triple-qualified persona, no money number, meta-premise instead of a demanded task.
6. (2/10) 47s runtime with payoff ~35s in — real but downstream; most viewers were gone by second 6.

## ⛔ PRE-BUILD KILL-RULES (run on every future premise BEFORE scripting)
1. **FRAME-1 RECEIPT test:** mock the actual screenshot of the artifact's output first. If a stranger can't decode "what it is + why it's valuable" from that frame in 2s, kill the premise. If the premise can't produce a real screen at all, it's dead (no exceptions for premium animation).
2. **CEREBRAL-PAYOFF auto-kill (mechanical):** if the payoff noun is insight/rules/judgment/analysis/map/patterns/blind-spots/profile, auto-kill without debate. (VAULT + WORLDLOAD both hit this; the gate already existed and was skipped.)
3. **INPUT-EXISTS test:** the thing the viewer feeds in must already exist on their machine or in their life (a folder, their chats, their setup). If they'd have to assemble or remember it first, kill.
4. **ONE-BREATH RESTATE:** premise must compress to "you give it X (that you have) → it hands you Y (a noun you can screenshot)". If the chain needs "and later, other models read it and...", it's 3+ hops — reframe until Y is the immediate output or kill.
5. **PROOF-SHOT test:** write the single shot that proves the promise (before/after, output, number). If the honest proof-shot would look like generic text, the promise is unprovable — kill.
6. **NO NEGATION IN THE FIRST BREATH:** never open with "the smartest move isn't…" — a concrete directive/value noun by ~word 12.
7. **LEVER ROTATION + REAL-URGENCY test:** never open two consecutive reels on the same urgency lever. Ask "does this reel still make sense without the deadline?" If yes, the deadline is set dressing — demote it out of line 1.
8. **STAKE-QUALIFIER count:** list the qualifiers a viewer needs to feel the stake. More than ONE qualifier = kill or broaden to a universal pain (money, time, being lied to, being replaceable).
9. **FIRST-ORDER TASK rule:** the premise must DO a task with existing demand (books, ads, jobs, editing, building, selling). Meta-premises about optimizing future AI use need a money receipt in the first 3s or they die.
