**HOOKS (highest consensus):**
- First ~3s decide everything. Hook SUPERSEDES concept — rewrite the whole video to get a better hook (Hoyos).
- **The FIRST SENTENCE is the hook — no setup/warm-up fluff (Alex, 2026-06-19).** Lead with the most intriguing, straight-to-the-point claim; don't spend a sentence setting it up before the punch lands. (e.g. NOT "This carousel looks like you paid a designer $500. Nobody did. Claude built it…" → YES "This carousel looks like a $500 designer made it — it was one Claude prompt.")
- **RECEIPTS-FIRST for capability/outcome hooks (2026-06-22, creator comparison).** When the payoff is a single NUMBER or a demonstrated RESULT, LEAD WITH IT — do NOT withhold it. The lane (Nate Herk demo-flex / Greg promise-restate / the "I Gave Claude My P&L" receipts format) opens on the proof; the open loop then becomes "HOW did it do that?" Reserve withhold-to-the-end for multi-step BUILD/PROCESS reels. Also require a NICHE SIGNATURE: a holdable architecture mental-model OR a credibility/comparison beat. Full reconciliation with the close-loop rule in [[reel-storytelling-playbook]]; creator hooks in [[nateherk-style-reference]].
- **⛔ LEAD THE SPOKEN SCRIPT WITH THE WORD "Claude" — like Nate Herk (Alex, 2026-06-23).** The very first words of the VO name the subject + the bold capability/result: "**Claude** just replaced half of what interior designers do", "**Claude** can now build $10k websites", "**Claude** just learned the 6 skills people get paid for". Do NOT bury Claude behind a warm-up opener ("Did you know you can…", "You can now…", "Ever wondered…") — start ON "Claude" (or the specific named tool/subject) so the hook + the keyword land in word one. Pairs with receipts-first (Claude + the result together) and the IG-SEO keyword rule in [[reel-hook-header]] (Claude spoken AND on-screen first).
- Hook must be self-explanatory ON MUTE — viewer knows what it's about with sound off (Hoyos). "Title+thumbnail test": would the opening earn a click as a long-form title/thumbnail? → the OPENING VISUAL must carry the hook, not just the spoken words.
- SPECIFICITY is the #1 hook lever (Mino Lee): details, NUMBERS, paint the exact dream result in the target viewer's own words. (= Alex's "no vague language" rule — "whole bet" BAD, "the new app I'm about to launch" GOOD.)
- Surprise with the UNUSUAL (Ship30). Ship30 4-step hook: (1) name the benefit, (2) put it in an UNEXPECTED setting (opposite of normal), (3) frame first-person ("I/My/How I"), (4) edit for clarity + credibility (will they know what to expect? is it believable?).

**TARGET-FIRST STRUCTURE (Mino Lee):** every script = (1) name the EXACT target viewer (a founder dodging a senior hire, not "people"), (2) name a SPECIFIC dream result for them, (3) deliver YOUR solution. Virality is a reverse-engineerable science, not luck ("synthesizing ideas" = combine proven angles).

**STRUCTURE & RETENTION (Hoyos, most operational):**
- "Hook, foreshadow, end": hook (≤3s) → immediately FORESHADOW what's coming incl. the conflict AND the expected payoff → body.
- **NAME THE THING YOU'RE REVEALING — frame it as a specific copyable setup, not a capability tour (Alex, 2026-06-20).** When the video teaches a workflow/system/hack/prompt, the foreshadow line must make clear you're SHOWING THEM A SPECIFIC, REPLICABLE THING — use a concrete noun for it (the **setup** / the **system** / the **workflow** / the **stack** / the **prompt**) and refer back to that noun. Flagged example: "Here's the three things it does while you're sleeping" was unclear — it reads like a tour of what Claude *can already do* (a capability list), not a setup the viewer can copy, and a vague "it" floats. Fix: "Here's the exact **setup** — three jobs **it** runs every night" (now "it" = the setup). This also anchors the comment-gate CTA: the gated asset is "the full **setup**," so the same noun must be established up front. RULE: if the foreshadow could be misheard as "cool things this tool does" instead of "here's the build I'm giving you," rename it with a concrete setup-noun. **(Verified 2026-06-20 by sourced deep-research — how the top AI/founder creators actually do it: Nate Herk, Greg Isenberg, Nick Saraev, Mino Lee, Hormozi.)** Framing principle = **turn the capability into a possessable NOUN, then hand it over**, in 3 moves: (1) **NAME + OWN it** as a discrete noun with authorship + a concreteness marker — **"exact" is the single highest-signal token** ("here's the **exact** system/workflow/prompt/setup", "the inbox agent **I built** in 25 minutes", optionally brand it "I call it the ___ system"); a capability stays a VERB ("Claude can summarize your inbox") while a copyable asset is a countable NOUN ("the exact 3-node setup I built"). (2) **SHOW it run** (demonstrate live, don't list). (3) **HAND IT OVER** via the comment/save gate. Match the noun's scope to what you give: prompt = one paste; system/workflow/setup/build = a larger scaffold. Other proven copyability frames (all high-commonness): **"Steal this / Copy this [named system]"** (possessive + proper name = transferable property); **contrarian pivot** "stop using Claude like [X], do this instead" / "it's not the tool, it's the system" (demotes raw capability, elevates YOUR scaffolding — the cleanest way to say "this isn't what the AI already does"); **outcome-promise** "by the end you'll have your own ___"; **finite count** ("the only 3 ___ you need") = bounded/reproducible.
- **PRODUCT-REPLACES-ROLE frame, NOT a viewer-scold (Alex, 2026-06-20).** For "replace a cost/role" topics, lead with the WORKFLOW/setup as the HERO that replaces an expensive role — **"This Claude workflow replaces a $72k-a-year marketer"** / "one Claude agent that does a $60k analyst's job for ~$13/day." Positive, screenshot-worthy proof; the subject is the BUILD + the result. Do NOT use the loss/accusation frame — **"You're about to spend $72k on a hire you don't need" / "you don't need" / "stop wasting money on ___"** (Alex flagged this exact phrasing) — it scolds/accuses/preaches at the viewer. Keep the number and the role; drop the accusation. Rule: the hero of the sentence is the workflow and what it replaces, never the viewer's supposed mistake.
- **LISTICLE NUMBERING = ONE CONSISTENT ORDINAL SERIES (Alex, 2026-06-20).** When the body lists multiple numbered items (e.g. "three jobs / three things / 3 prompts"), label them with a SINGLE consistent ordinal set — **First, Second, Third** — not mixed connectors ("First… Then… And…" is inconsistent and reads sloppy). If you announce a count ("three jobs"), each item must be clearly marked as #1/#2/#3 in the same form so the viewer can track the list. (Keep them tight — the ordinal is the only structural word; don't also stack other rule-of-three tells.) (Sourced 2026-06-20: a FINITE count is itself a copyability signal — "the only 12 ___", "7 ___ workflows" — so STATE the count in the hook, then pay it off with visible sequential ordinals (1/2/3 or step 1/step 2) in order; Hormozi's documented rule — make the numbered items actionable DO-THIS steps, not topic labels. Strict count==items-delivered is good craft but only thinly sourced; the well-attested part is: announce the count → deliver sequential ordinals → items are actions.)
- End each beat the instant you deliver it. CUT ALL DEAD AIR (= Alex's no-static-stretches + zero-leading-silence rules).
- "BUT / SO" loop, NEVER "and then": set up conflict → resolve → set up new conflict. Connector discipline holds tension (Parker/Stone but-therefore rule).
- Pacing arc: fast hook → medium middle → FAST PEAK → medium resolution → fast ending. Make the PEAK and the ENDING the strongest beats (Peak-End: people remember the peak + the end).
- ~34s sweet spot so 90%+ retention is reachable (her heuristic; sub-30s needs rewatch/>100%).

**RETENTION CHECKLISTS:**
- Hormozi "reward the consumer": content must (1) give a reason to watch [hook], (2) hold attention [retention], (3) satisfy that reason [payoff]. Any broken link = they leave.
- Colin & Samir 3 rules: (1) earn the click, (2) respect their time — deliver on the promise; first 30s decide if they stay, (3) "do you want more like this?" — convert to repeat viewers.
- Ernesto Perez (via C&S): "Stop, Hook, Payoff."

**DELIVERY:** "Not just WHAT you say but HOW you say it" (Mino Lee) — tonality/energy matters as much as words; learnable via reps. Relevant when Alex records his own VO.

**CTA / SELLING (refined 2026-06-19 — the Nate-Herk short-form move Alex likes):** value-FIRST, then a **STRONG comment-to-access gate**. Deliver a genuinely useful slice IN the reel (the what + a real taste of the how), then entice HARD to comment a keyword to unlock the FULL / done-for-you version (the complete prompt, the full template, the whole system, the step-by-step). The gate is the climax. Still honors "soft-sell, never hard-sell" (Mino Lee — "the instant viewers feel pitched, they scroll") because you're trading a FREE, more-complete asset for a comment, NOT pitching a paid product. Two rules so it never feels cringe: (1) the reel must deliver REAL value first, not just a tease; (2) the gated XYZ must be the natural COMPLETE version of exactly what you just showed. (Reconciles Mino Lee soft-sell + the panel "CTA trades a NEW asset".)

**PACKAGING / IDEA (Galloway — long-form, apply by analogy):** CCN test — the idea must work for a Core, Casual, AND New viewer. Idea/hook > production quality. Decide the hook + opening visual BEFORE building the video.

**DOCUMENTATION & VOLUME (for scaling the stream):** GaryVee reverse pyramid (one pillar → 30+ platform-native micro-pieces → drive back to pillar); Justin Welsh Content OS / Hub-and-Spoke (hub video/newsletter → 6-12 spokes); Hormozi volume. Treat long-form Claude reels/threads as pillars to atomize.

**DISAGREE — judgment calls:** the #1 lever (packaging vs raw reward vs hook-mechanics; complementary, not contradictory); exact retention math is format-specific; most frameworks are long-form/written-first → adapt, don't copy verbatim.

**APPLY-TO-EVERY-SCRIPT CHECKLIST:** (1) name the exact target founder + a specific dream result; (2) hook in first 3s — specific + numeric + understandable on mute, opening VISUAL carries it; (3) foreshadow the payoff right after the hook; (4) build the body as but/so conflict loops — kill "and then"; (5) end each beat on delivery, zero dead air; (6) pace fast→med→FAST PEAK→med→fast, strongest peak + ending; (7) every line rewards the viewer (reason→attention→payoff); (8) soft comment-gated CTA + convert to repeat; (9) no vague nouns (specificity); (10) distinct visual metaphor per beat (existing rule); (11) **LENGTH = 30-45s, ~90-120 spoken words** (Alex's standing preference, confirmed 2026-06-19) — cut hard to the single strongest beat; don't let human/story detail bloat past 45s (~2.7 words/sec).

**OVERRIDE (2026-06-19): NO anecdotes / story framing — see [[no-anecdote-value-first-scripts]].** Alex rejected the "human protagonist with a wound" + "quantify via a human reaction" approach as gimmicky filler. Deliver value DIRECTLY: the prompt, the steps, the concrete result, addressed to "you". Keep the dollar numbers, but state them as facts/results, NOT wrapped in an invented character ("Maya", "a guy paying $40") or an "I did X" story. Apply the OTHER panel upgrades below (escalation, loop ending, reading-rule, chopped peak, CTA-trades-asset, tonality) WITHOUT the narrative wrapper.

**PANEL UPGRADES (2026-06-19, 6-creator-lens workflow — apply on top of the checklist; cross-lens consensus first):**
- **QUANTIFY THE COST OF INACTION** with a specific dollar figure tied to ONE human reaction, and make that number the PEAK (placed last). "Finds where you undercharge" → "found $1,920/yr from one customer who said yes in 9 minutes." Nothing is screenshot-worthy without a number. (3 lenses)
- **MAKE STAKES VIVID/VISCERAL, and CUT DECORATIVE FLUFF (Alex, 2026-06-19).** Describe the loss/payoff with concrete physical imagery, not flat verbs: "you're dropping four thousand dollars on the ground every year" beats "you're undercharging by $4k." AND cut flourish phrases that add no information — Alex flagged "the exact sentence to say so you can raise prices without your voice shaking" as unnecessary; keep it functional ("the exact words to say when you send it"). Vivid on the stakes, lean on the mechanics.
- **PROTAGONIST = A PERSON WITH A WOUND, not the prompt or a generic "you."** Track one founder from fear→relief; the prompt is the catalyst, not the hero. Founders share because they felt SEEN, not because they learned a technique. (3 lenses)
- **BAIT THE STAKE, NOT THE TOOL.** "How to prompt Claude better" is a Core-only ceiling. Reframe frame-1 on their money/decision/blind-spot so it travels to casual+new viewers; burn a mute-readable number/contrast on frame 1. (3 lenses)
- **FIX THE ENDING — loop or emotional aftermath, never a resolved thesis** (a tidy conclusion is an exit ramp). Loop the last line back to the opening number to manufacture rewatch, OR land the felt human moment ("starts feeling like the co-founder you couldn't afford"). (2 lenses)
- **ESCALATE THE BODY — rising-stakes ladder, never flat parallel items.** Re-order beats by consequence and SAY the climb ("that saves a bad launch; this saves a customer; but the third saves the whole company"). (2 lenses)
- Give the **JUDGMENT/reading-rule, not just the prompt** (the prompt is a commodity they can write; the threshold/"if X then Y" for reading the output is the real screenshot).
- **Self-selecting tripwire hook:** stage + symptom + this-week timing in the first 5 words ("if you haven't touched pricing since launch and you're scared to email a new number…") instead of a generic "you" or a budget bracket that excludes most viewers.
- **Chop the peak into short fragments** so cut-rhythm accelerates into the climax (15-word clause → 2-4 word clauses). Make the peak FELT on mute.
- **CTA trades a NEW asset** the video didn't already give (fill-in template, scoring rubric, multi-step version) — commenting buys something bigger than the ask.
- **NO FLUFF AT THE VERY END — keep the CTA to ONE tight line; refer to the gated asset with a SINGLE noun, don't list its contents (Alex, 2026-06-20).** The last line is a peak-end retention beat, so it must be the tightest in the script. Do NOT tack a contents-list onto the CTA. Flagged: "Comment NIGHT and I'll send you the whole setup — the schedule, the prompt, and the exact tools" → trim to "**Comment NIGHT and I'll send you the whole setup.**" The list ("the schedule, the prompt, and the tools") adds seconds, dribbles off the energy, and hurts watch-time/loop. One asset-noun ("the whole setup" / "the full template" / "the system") is enough — viewers already know roughly what's in it from the body. End on the keyword + the single-noun promise, then stop. **(Sourced 2026-06-20:)** canonical form = **"Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM you the [one-noun asset]"** — three parts only: asset → ONE single-word keyword tied to the asset (SETUP / AGENT / BUILD / PROMPT / NIGHT) → instant-delivery verb (DM/send). Shortest: "Comment SETUP and I'll DM you the whole thing." Optional save-stack for cold audiences: "Save this, then comment SETUP and I'll send you the exact setup." Compress the asset to one noun/pronoun ("the whole thing" / "the setup" / "it"); push any contents detail to the auto-DM or the FIRST caption line, never the spoken CTA. Place it in the last 2-3s. Verified verbatim forms: Brock Johnson "Comment HOOK and I'll send you the 25 viral hook ideas"; Tracy Harmoush "Comment 'REST' and I'll DM you the full conversation."
- **Notate the on-camera read** ([pause]/[slow down]/[drop volume]) and deliver the PEAK slower + quieter than the lines around it, so restraint sells and the soft-sell never flips to a pitch.

**HUMANIZE THE SCRIPT — sound human, never AI (2026-06-19 research; apply to EVERY script, esp. anything Claude drafts):**
- **KILL AI vocab.** Tier-1 (humans ~never say): delve, tapestry, landscape, pivotal, underscore, testament, intricate, meticulous, nuanced, multifaceted, embark, spearhead, bolster, garner, realm, beacon, cornerstone, frontier. Tier-2 (don't overuse): leverage, seamless, robust, foster, enhance, navigate, resonate, showcase, holistic, comprehensive, innovative, dynamic, cutting-edge, game-changer, harness, unleash, unlock, elevate, unveil, revolutionize, synergy, vibrant, crucial. Use the plain word: "use" not leverage, "dig into" not delve, "improve" not elevate.
- **KILL stock phrases:** "in today's world", "in the realm of", "the world of", "when it comes to", "let's dive in / unpack / break this down", "it's worth noting", "here's the thing", "the part nobody catches", "in conclusion".
- **KILL audible structural tells (stronger than vocab):** "it's not X, it's Y" / not-X-but-Y negation; robotic rule-of-three/tricolons; balanced A-B mirror sentences; rhetorical-question-then-immediate-answer; dramatic "here's the thing" reveals; every line landing on a neat button.
- **ADD human cadence (burstiness):** vary sentence length HARD — long line, then short, then a fragment. Never an even 15-25-word meter. Start some sentences with And/But. Use fragments. Leave a thought slightly unresolved. Real numbers/names/dates. Let an opinion show. Mild asymmetry is good. **BUT don't over-stack short choppy fragments (Alex flagged 2026-06-19 — "No Canva. No designer. One prompt." reads too staccato/robotic): at most ONE short punch in a row, never three; fold the punchy bits INTO flowing sentences ("...in five minutes, with no Canva and no design skills") rather than machine-gunning standalone fragments.**
- **DINNER-TABLE TEST:** read every line aloud — if you wouldn't actually say it to a friend at dinner, cut or rewrite it. Spoken scripts only keep words you'd really say.
- **CONTEXT-MATCHED COMMON PHRASING (Alex, 2026-06-20):** use the plain, everyday phrase that fits the LITERAL context — never swap in a less-common idiom or a phrase that means something else in context. Flagged example: in a sleep/overnight script, "while you're out" is WRONG ("out" reads as *out of the house*, not asleep) → use "while you're sleeping" / "overnight" / "while you sleep." Pick the words a normal person would actually use for that exact situation; if a phrase is uncommon OR could be misread in context, replace it with the obvious natural one. (Sub-rule of the dinner-table test — check every idiom against the scene it's describing.)
- **META (most important):** the fix is RESTRAINT + TASTE, not blanket-banning. AI's real tell is the robotically CONSISTENT deployment of devices — so RATION them, vary them, break the symmetry. Don't strip all rhythm; make it human-uneven.
- **Quantified pre-ship check:** 0 Tier-1 words; Tier-2 ≤1 each; ≤2 formal transitions total; 0 "not-X-but-Y"; ≤1 robotic tricolon; ≥30% of lines DON'T end on a neat button; ≥1 And/But opener. (Em-dash overuse is a TEXT tell — for spoken scripts, hunt AUDIBLE tells.)
- **To generate in-voice:** few-shot prime Claude with 4+ of Alex's real pieces analyzed for voice/rhythm + ban-list the words above. Refresh the vocab list periodically — tells drift by model generation (e.g. "delve" peaked 2023-24).

**⭐ CURRENT PREFERRED REEL STYLE (Alex, 2026-06-23 — for "Claude can now X" capability reels; overrides the "deliver the steps in the reel" lean): NATE-HERK GATED + FOMO.** Alex iterated to this after rejecting both my generic framework scripts AND the deliver-everything how-to versions. The formula:
1. **Direct, claim-forward hook** — present-tense capability + a number/$/multiplier, modeled on Nate Herk's REAL Short titles: "Andrej Karpathy Just 10x'd Everyone's Claude Code", "Seedance 2.0 + Claude Code = Beautiful $10k Websites", "How to Use Claude Code for 99% CHEAPER", "...is Finally Here", "...Just Changed [X] Forever". Get to the point immediately ("Claude can now make IG carousels" energy) — no slow tease. (The IG carousel-reel creator's hook also works: "Did you know Claude can now [capability] without [pain]? So before you [hard old way], try this. People are literally [result] in [time]." — see [[claude-reel-hook-library]].)
2. **HYPE the features hard throughout** — "here's where it gets ridiculous/crazy", "and it's not a rough draft", stack what it does in escalating beats.
3. **Stack FOMO** — contrast "people are running their whole X off this" vs "everyone else is still [painful old way] at midnight", "you're already falling behind", "the unfair advantage right now".
4. **⛔ GATE THE HOW — keep mechanics VAGUE.** Do NOT give the literal steps/prompt in the spoken reel (that closes the loop + kills the reason to comment). Describe WHAT it does and the OUTCOME; withhold HOW. The real value is delivered in the gated DM. **This supersedes [[no-anecdote-value-first-scripts]]'s "deliver the prompt/steps directly" for these capability reels** — value is still real, just gated, not free in the audio.
5. **CTA = a genuinely strong reason to comment** — "I put the exact prompt + the full setup in a free doc. Comment KEYWORD and I'll send it to your DMs." Add a withheld sweetener if natural ("plus the one tweak that makes it sound like you, not AI").
6. **⛔ NO HYPE-TAIL AFTER THE CTA (Alex, 2026-06-23).** He cut "it's the same system people are using to fill a month before lunch. Don't sleep on this one." END on the keyword + what they get, then STOP. (Reinforces the existing "NO FLUFF AT THE VERY END" CTA rule above.)
7. **LENGTH ~130-150 words / ~45-50s.** A 208-word version clocked ~75s — too long; trim hard. (Measure: `wc -w`; ~2.7 words/sec.)
8. **⛔ KEEP IN-SCRIPT LISTS SHORT — 2-3 items, never 4+ (Alex, 2026-06-25: "if you're going to talk multiple in a list then only have a few, not so many").** When the VO rattles off examples (tasks it does, things you give it, etc.), pick the 2-3 STRONGEST/most relatable and stop — a 4+ item list rattles on, drags the pace, and reads as filler. Flagged: "filling out forms, pulling numbers off a dozen dashboards, checking every competitor's pricing, cleaning up your tabs" (4) → trim to 3.

**TOFU BEAT — explain WHY it matters for unfamiliar concepts (Alex, 2026-06-23).** When the topic leans on a concept the viewer might not already value (a lead magnet, MCP, Claude Skills, an AI Council, a custom agent), add ONE quick beat early that says, in plain terms, what it is and the value it gives — e.g. "a lead magnet is the free thing you trade for an email; it's how you build an audience you actually own." That makes the reel TOFU (top-of-funnel) so it reaches people who don't already know the concept, not just insiders. Keep it to 1–2 lines — educate, don't lecture — then return to the hype/gated structure. Self-evident topics (clone a website, rewrite my landing page) don't need it. Note: NEW/frontier workflows usually DO need a TOFU beat precisely because the audience isn't familiar yet — pairs with the freshness rule in [[claude-reel-topic-engine]].
