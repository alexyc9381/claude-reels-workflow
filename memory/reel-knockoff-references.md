Alex (2026-07-10, during the SLASH two-screen build): "reference real things and do knockoffs whenever possible in the scenes… a soda should be a Coke, a financial dashboard = knockoff Robinhood's UI and logo and name, a cruise = knockoff Royal Caribbean… characters too, like add Iron Man if there's random strangers walking by… more scene interaction, like seagulls flying into buildings if it's a beach, or a talking tree… and try to add in real meme videos (he'll supply meme-video libraries)… use more boom pop and sparkle type sound effects and different sound effects."

**Why:** generic props read boring; a recognizable-but-parodied real thing is instantly legible AND funny — it's the same principle as the real-assets rule ([[behance-real-images]]) extended to fictionalized brands and character cameos.

**How to apply (every reel):**
1. **Every prop that HAS a famous real-world counterpart gets the knockoff of it** — named + styled close enough to recognize in 0.5s: soda→"Coke-ish" red can w/ white ribbon; finance dashboard→"Robinhood-ish" green feather app; insurance→"GEKKO insurance" (green + gecko mascot); telco→"Vorizon" red-check; cruise→"Royal Caribbean-ish". Bills/statements already do this (xfinity-style bill in SLASH — Alex approved the pattern).
2. **Character cameos:** background walk-ons/fly-bys of parody famous characters (pixel Iron-Man-ish hero flyby, etc.) as blink-and-miss gags — 1-2 per reel, background layer, never covering the hero (occlusion rule still applies).
3. **Scene interactions / living-world gags:** props react to the scene (seagull hits the building, a tree comments, sprites sweat/cry/cheer). 2-3 micro-gags per reel.
4. **Meme videos:** Alex will provide meme-video libraries — reels can embed real meme clips in the practical screen (Remotion `<OffthreadVideo>`); leave a slot for them where a reaction beat fits. PENDING his library.
5. **SFX:** lean harder on boom/pop/sparkle-type hits and VARY the palette per beat (lib_boom, lib_pop/pop2, sparkle, ding, lib_cinematic_hit, crowd_cheer + the [[sfx-library]] categories) — not the same whoosh everywhere.
