# Creator-lane ceilings (arena-confirmed, 2026-07-12)

Two head-to-head arena rounds (8 candidates, then 4 raycfu/nicksaraev challengers vs the champion), 3 independent judges each, scoring hook · believability · breadth · artifact/receipt · transfer · freshness. Result: **the same formula won twice, and the challenger lanes lost on structure, not wording.**

## ⭐ THE FORMULA THAT WINS (confirmed twice)
> **Point Claude at a thing ANYONE ALREADY HAS → watch a real working artifact build itself → hand over a shareable link.**
Napkin, screenshot, blank tab, a pile of photos. Three requirements: the **input is universal**, the **artifact is ADDITIVE and mute-legible in <2s**, and the **payoff is an object the viewer wants to send to someone else**.
Champion: **SKETCH** (8.5, unanimous) — draw an app on a napkin, snap one photo, get a live clickable link. Runner-up GAME (7.3) is its structural twin (same one-prompt→watch→link chassis) — do NOT ship both back to back.

## ⛔ THE DISCRIMINATOR: the artifact must BE the payoff
Every losing script's climax was a **proposition**, not an object:
- score going DOWN = viewer holds distrust (HOMEWORK)
- a picture of a badge (CERT)
- a SUBTRACTION — proving the old tool is gone never proves the new one works (CANCEL)
- a counter at ZERO (BURN)
If the reel ends on an absence, a number that drops, or a claim, it loses to any reel that ends on a thing you can hold and send.

## raycfu lane → caps on BREADTH (the engine, not a defect)
"Most people don't realize [X]" / insider-gap / "everybody's doing X wrong" **only fires on a viewer who already holds the wrong belief**, so the ceiling IS the size of the in-group. Proof: the purest insider-frame script finished LAST in both rounds, on the same breadth axis that killed round 1's bottom three. And **HOMEWORK scored 7.2 precisely to the extent it smuggled itself OUT of the lane** ("your AI grades its own homework" is a universal stake, not an insider correction).
**Best use:** mid-funnel retention for people who ALREADY follow the account; a "you're doing it wrong" reel farms argument comments on a warm audience. **Never the reach play. Never the cold-scroller converter.**

## nicksaraev lane → caps on BELIEVABILITY (not breadth)
The claim SHAPE is fine. Two structural problems: (1) his natural claims ("one file replaces Zapier", "$X/mo → $0") are **the most farmed sentences in AI content**, so scam-smell fires before the receipt lands; (2) his receipts are **subtractive** (a cancelled bill, a saved cost) rather than additive (a thing that now exists). Also the money frame **quietly re-imports the killed business TAM** — a student/parent has no subscription to cancel, so the outro writes a check the premise can't cash. See [[brand-factory-log]]: BRAND capped at believability 7 / breadth 6-7 for exactly this reason.
**Best use: a SPICE, not a spine.** Borrow the number density + the hard on-screen figure and staple them onto a breadth-safe artifact reel. Do NOT build the premise on the dollar sign.

## Routing rule going forward
1. Pick an **artifact-first premise** with a universal input FIRST (that's the spine).
2. Then borrow parts: raycfu's hook mechanics, nicksaraev's number density, cindiezhu's persona-naming + "which means" outcome-translation.
3. Do not audition an insider-frame or money-receipt premise as the headline reel — it loses to any artifact-first reel on the same board.
Also standing (Alex, 2026-07-12): when replicating any creator, keep their STRUCTURE + number-density but **strip their on-camera verbal tics** (Nick's "Clean. Actually clean." doubles, vague words like "vibe") — they read as filler/AI-slop in a faceless VO.
