⛔ **NEVER build reel captions from raw whisper `-ml 1` word times.** Alex has flagged "captions aren't synced / WHY AGAIN" on the listicle reel, the carousel reel, AND the $297 offer reel — every time the cause was the SAME shortcut: trusting whisper's per-word `from` times directly. Whisper word starts are biased per-line by **±0.1 to 0.4s** (scatter, not a constant), so some lines lag the voice = desync.

**Why it keeps happening:** under time pressure I skip the measured-onset step and just dump whisper times into `words_*.json`. STOP doing that. It is faster to do it right once than to re-render after Alex catches it.

**MANDATORY method before EVERY render (the only acceptable way):**
1. **Measure each caption LINE's real speech onset from the WAV** (`public/vo_*.wav`) — numpy RMS in 10ms windows (floor/speech percentile → midpoint threshold; find the quiet→loud rising edge in a ±0.25s window around the whisper time), or `silencedetect` for gapped lines. (whisper.cpp `-dtw` is disabled on this Mac.)
2. **Anchor the line's first word to that measured onset**; offset the rest of the line by the same delta (CONSTANT-offset per line — preserves whisper's within-line relative spacing). Cap a suspicious |delta|>~0.25 and carry the previous line's delta for continuous (no-gap) lines.
3. **Global LEAD ≈ 0.10s** (subtract) so captions land slightly BEFORE the word and NEVER lag — this also absorbs the MP4 AAC-priming / real-player shift (a real player plays the MP4 audio ~0.05-0.1s earlier than the embedded timestamps, so MP4-frame extraction looks "synced" while the real player lags; always measure from the WAV, not the MP4).
4. **Drift gate:** for every visible line assert `|caption.start − measured_onset|` is small; fix outliers before render.
5. **Frame-verify** at 2-3 measured word onsets (extract the rendered frame, confirm the spoken word is already on screen).

⛔ **WHISPER HALLUCINATES WORDS INTO LONG SILENCES → totally broken timing (reel 15 SEO, Alex 2026-06-26: "audio is completely broken at the beginning, I hear no audio").** If the recording has a big internal DEAD-AIR gap (false start, then a multi-second pause, then the real take), whisper (faster-whisper AND whisper.cpp) will INVENT the spoken words spread across the silence — so the transcript reads fine but every word time is fiction, and the rendered reel has seconds of silent video while captions/scenes play to phantom audio. Reel 15's VO had a 0.05s blip then a **6.4s silence** (0.1→6.56s) then the real take; whisper placed "Did you know Claude can now steal…" across the 0-6.5s silence. **HARD PRE-BUILD CHECK: run `silencedetect=noise=-34dB:d=0.4` on the spliced VO and look for any silence_duration > ~1.5s. If found, that's dead air, not speech — trim it out (`atrim=start=<real_speech_start>`) and RE-TRANSCRIBE before building words/L.** The tell: whisper word durations stretch absurdly (e.g. "you" 0.64→1.98s) and the "hook" spans 10s. Confirm the speech is contiguous (only natural ~0.4-0.6s sentence pauses remain) before trusting any timing.

⛔ **LEAD-SILENCE GLOBAL DESYNC (reel 14, Alex 2026-06-26: "slight pause at the very beginning… start with me immediately saying the first word").** If `words_*.json[0].start ≈ 0.0` (speech-relative) but the WAV still has leading silence before the first word, and `<Audio src=… />` mounts at frame 0 with NO `trimBefore`/`startFrom` and `Captions` uses `fr(w.start)` directly — then the ENTIRE audio lags the captions + scene-changes by that lead the whole reel, AND there's dead air at the start. Reel 14's `vo_ads.wav` had **0.47s** lead (silencedetect: silence_end 0.4707) while words.json/L were speech-relative (confirmed: last-word 49.9 + 0.47 lead ≈ 50.44 wav-end). FIX = trim the lead off the WAV so the wav matches the speech-relative data: `ffmpeg -i vo.wav -af "atrim=start=0.46,asetpts=PTS-STARTPTS" -c:a pcm_s16le out.wav` (trim ~0.01s SHORT of silence_end so the word's attack isn't clipped), then re-render — no need to touch words.json/L/duration. So the BUILD step must either trim the wav lead OR mount Audio with `trimBefore={fr(lead)}`; verify wav speech-onset ≈ words.json[0].start before shipping.

Also: fix whisper mishears in `words_*.json` before render (it writes "cloud" for "Claude", lowercases, drops words). Reference impl: the python in the 2026-06-21 session → `ClaudeOfferReel` `words_offer.json`. Full detail in [[claude-ai-reel-workflow]] lessons (h)(i)(j) — this file is the BLUNT GATE so it never gets skipped again.

**⛔ LINE-SWITCH GATE (added 2026-07-05, from WORTHY v3.2 — Alex flagged word ENDS getting cut off):** anchoring word STARTS is not enough. A caption line must not switch to the next line until the previous line's last word has finished SOUNDING, or sentence-final words get visually cut mid-speech whenever the next line starts hot (e.g. "used," starts 11.04, next line "but" starts 11.12). Standard gate: `lineN+1 takes over at max(lineN+1.start, min(lineN.end + 0.05, lineN+1.start + 0.5))` — the 0.5s cap guards against whisper's inflated end-times. Reference impl: Captions component in ClaudeWorthyReel.tsx.

**⛔ SILENCEREMOVE EATS QUIET TRAILING WORDS (2026-07-05, WORTHY v5; Alex flagged twice before root cause found):** `-35dB peak` classifies quiet sentence-final words as silence and physically removes them from the VO ("used" was left with ~60ms). STANDING: use **stop_threshold=-45dB, stop_duration=0.6** in every VO splice chain. VERIFY BEFORE BUILD: window-transcribe 5s around 2-3 sentence-final words in the FINAL wav and check each has a plausible duration (>0.2s) + a gap before the next word; if a word shows <0.15s, the chain clipped it. Whisper full-file times can HIDE this (they smear); only the window transcription shows it.
