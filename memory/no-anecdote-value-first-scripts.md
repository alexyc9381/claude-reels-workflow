Alex's standing rule (set 2026-06-19): reel scripts must **deliver the actual value directly** — the prompt, the steps, the concrete result. Do NOT build a script around story/anecdote framing.

Specifically BANNED:
- First-person personal narrative as the spine ("I ran 1,000 prompts", "I called the guy that afternoon", "I sent one email").
- Invented characters / named anecdotes ("a founder I know", "Maya", "a guy paying forty bucks a month").
- "Let me tell you what happened" story arcs (fear → turn → relief).

**Why:** Alex found the anecdote-heavy rewrites (the "human protagonist with a wound" direction) gimmicky and filler — he wants straight value, not a personal story. The invented anecdotes also aren't even true.

**How to apply:** Center the VALUE and teach it. Give the exact prompt/steps and state the concrete result as fact. Talk TO the viewer ("you" / "here's how" / "the move is"), instructional — not "here's what happened to me." Keep "I" minimal and only when genuinely needed, never as a story device. Keep the concrete NUMBERS for the payoff, but state them as a result/fact ("that's usually a few grand a year you're leaving on the table"), NOT wrapped in an anecdote. Still keep HUMAN CADENCE (burstiness, plain spoken voice, no AI tells) — sounding human ≠ telling personal stories.

This **OVERRIDES** the earlier panel upgrades "make the protagonist a person with a wound" and "quantify tied to a single human reaction" in [[shortform-scripting-playbook]]. Keep the other panel upgrades (escalation, loop ending, reading-rule, chopped peak, CTA-trades-a-new-asset, tonality marks) — just without the narrative wrapper. Reinforces "Scripts = STRAIGHT VALUE, no filler" in [[claude-ai-reel-workflow]].
