**STANDING RULE: tighten every reel's VO pacing** (Alex, 2026-06-22 — wants less dead air + faster delivery to help 2-second retention). Two mechanisms, applied at different stages:

**1) Global speedup ~7% (pitch-preserved), every reel.** Alex's natural VO ≈ 2.7 words/sec → ~2.9 at 7% — still crisp + clearly intelligible. 8% is fine for punchier; **~10–12% is the ceiling** before it sounds rushed and captions flash too fast to read. **Protect the value/payoff lines** (the prompt/result the viewer came for) — don't let the speedup rush those. The truly best "faster" is recording with more pace/energy (Nate Herk / Ray Fu deliver fast + confident) — post-speedup is a shortcut, see [[nateherk-style-reference]].

**2) Pause-trim — WHERE it happens depends on whether the reel is already built:**
- **FUTURE reels (BUILD time — clean, preferred):** trim the VO's inter-sentence pauses to ~0.25–0.35s **BEFORE** deriving the reel's timing. Detect silence (numpy RMS 10ms windows, or ffmpeg `silencedetect`), cap any gap > ~0.40s down to ~0.30s, rebuild the VO (concat speech segments + fixed gaps), THEN run the [[caption-sync-gate]] onset-anchoring + set `L[]` / `durationInFrames` off the *tight* VO. Apply the ~7% speedup to that VO too. Clean because captions + scene onsets + animation beats are all derived from the already-tight audio.
- **FINISHED reels (retrofit — what the backlog got):** you **CANNOT** surgically cut pauses out of a rendered reel — the animations are timed to the VO and keep moving during pauses, so cutting silent gaps would desync or jump them. Instead apply a **uniform ~7% speedup to the whole mp4** (video + audio scaled together → captions/animations stay perfectly synced, pitch preserved). This also shrinks every pause by 7%. Command (project ffmpeg-static):
  `ffmpeg -i in.mp4 -filter_complex "[0:v]setpts=PTS/1.07[v];[0:a]atempo=1.07[a]" -map "[v]" -map "[a]" -r 30 -c:v libx264 -pix_fmt yuv420p -crf 18 -c:a aac -b:a 192k out.mp4`
  Applied to the whole backlog 2026-06-22 (37.9s reel → 35.4s). For deeper pause-compression on a finished reel you'd need variable-speed segment splicing (speed silent segments ~2x, speech ~1.07x, concat) — more work/risk; only if Alex asks.

Speed is a **testable** variable but judge it across multiple posts with real view counts, not one cold-start reel (same trap as hook-retention noise — see [[reel-hook-header]]). Ties to [[claude-ai-reel-workflow]] (cut-dead-air / peak-end pacing) + [[shortform-scripting-playbook]].

**⛔ STATIC / NOISE from over-processing the VO (Alex, 2026-07-08 ARENA: "the audio has so much static").** The polish chain that caused it: `highshelf=g=3:f=7500` (treble boost amplifies mic hiss) + `acompressor ratio=3` (pulls the noise floor UP between words) + `loudnorm=I=-15` (loud target amplifies everything), all on a HOT recording (raw peak −0.1 dB, nearly clipping). Result: noise floor jumped from −62 dB (raw) to −48 dB = audible static. **Fix / clean VO chain (use this, verify with `astats`):** `highpass=f=85, afftdn=nr=12:nf=-45, acompressor=threshold=-20dB:ratio=2.2:attack=15:release=250, loudnorm=I=-17:TP=-2:LRA=12` — i.e. **denoise (afftdn), gentle compression, conservative loudnorm (I=-17, TP=-2 for headroom), and NO treble/highshelf boost.** Always run `ffmpeg -i vo.wav -af astats -f null -` and check **Noise floor should stay ≈ −60 dB or lower and Peak ≤ −2 dB**; if the noise floor climbed >10 dB vs the raw, you over-processed.

**⛔⛔ FORCE 48kHz ON THE VO WAV — afftdn/loudnorm silently UPSAMPLE to 192kHz (Alex, 2026-07-08 ARENA: "the voiceover keeps cutting out and lagging").** Root cause: my clean chain (`afftdn`/`loudnorm` with no explicit output `-ar`) wrote the VO at **192000 Hz**. The SFX files are 44.1/48kHz, so Remotion mixing a 192kHz VO against standard-rate SFX GLITCHED = the voice cut out + lagged. (vo_mint.wav is 192kHz too — same latent bug.) **ALWAYS end the VO build with an explicit `-ar 48000 -ac 1 -sample_fmt s16`, and verify with `ffprobe -select_streams a -show_entries stream=sample_rate` before rendering.** Standard rate for every `public/vo_*.wav` = **48000 Hz**. Also deliver the final mp4 with `-ar 48000 -ac 2` on the AAC.

**⛔ ALWAYS add `-movflags +faststart` to the delivery encode (Alex, 2026-07-08 ARENA: "still extremely glitched / lagging" even after the 48kHz fix).** The decoded audio was technically CLEAN (0 clicks, 0 clipping, in sync) — the "glitch/lag" was PLAYBACK stutter because the mp4's `moov` atom sat at the END of the file (no faststart), so a streaming player (Google Drive preview, etc.) has to load the whole file before it plays smoothly. **Verify** the moov is at the front: `python3 -c "d=open('f.mp4','rb').read(200000); print(d.find(b'moov')<d.find(b'mdat'))"`. Full delivery encode: `-c:v libx264 -pix_fmt yuv420p -crf 18 -c:a aac -b:a 192k -ar 48000 -ac 2 -movflags +faststart`. (Diagnose "glitched" complaints by DECODING the delivered audio to wav + numpy click/clip/dropout check BEFORE assuming re-record; Alex's raw takes peak ~0 dBFS = hot but usable, not the cause.) Secondary contributor: loud crowd/boom SFX beds sitting UNDER the VO can mask it and read as "cutting out" — keep VO-overlapping SFX beds ≤ ~0.2 and short.

**⛔ DON'T over-speed a fast take (Alex, 2026-07-08 ARENA: "wayyy too fast, sped up too much").** The ~7% speedup assumes a ~2.7 wps natural delivery. If the RECORDING is already brisk (ARENA's hook was ~5 wps), adding 1.07x on top makes it frantic. GATE the speedup on the take's natural pace: brisk take → **1.0x (no speedup)** or even slightly slower; only apply ~1.05–1.07x to a relaxed/slow take. When you change the VO speed on a not-yet-final reel, re-derive `L[]` beats + re-align captions + `durationInFrames` off the new-speed VO; scenes authored to old frame-counts just hold their payoff at the end (fine) since they use clamped `over()`/`interpolate()`.

**⛔ MUSIC-BED LEVEL — keep it ~0.18–0.25 UNDER the VO (Alex, 2026-06-23: "the background music is too loud compared to the voiceover").** The whole backlog shipped with music beds at **0.8–0.95** against a VO at default 1.0 → the music drowned the voice. FIX: VO (`vo_*.wav`) stays at default 1.0 — NEVER lower it; set the music `<Audio>` `volume` to **0.25 for normal-mastered beds** (`music_bed`/`music_md`/`music_ask`/`music_offer`/`ados_bed`) and **0.18 for loud masters** (`ados_bed_loud.wav`, ~0.2 with the ducking-interpolate form). Audio is baked into the render, so changing the mix means a full re-render of the reel — fold the music fix into any other re-render (hero/header) so you don't render twice. Standing default for ALL new reels: music bed ≤ 0.25.
