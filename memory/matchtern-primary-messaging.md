Matchtern's primary message is **"stand out in admissions"**: real, verifiable work experience at a venture-backed company is what makes a high schooler's application stand out to top colleges.

**Why:** As of 2026-06-16 Alex retired the old "High School Internships in 10 Days, Guaranteed" hook as the primary angle. Do not lead with "10 days guaranteed" in new ads/copy unless Alex revives it.

**How to apply:** Lead with the stand-out / "real work beats a perfect transcript" outcome, not a speed or guarantee claim. Still avoid fabricated stats; a hero-number ad waits until there's a defensible Matchtern number (placement count, admit rate). Used by the ads in [[matchtern-static-ad-pipeline]].

**Premium positioning — never frame Matchtern on price.** Alex flagged that "Real beats expensive" psychologically downgrades the brand. Do not position Matchtern as the cheaper/better-value option; it is premium. You may disparage competitors' price (e.g. "$8k pay-to-play camps") but never imply Matchtern wins on being cheaper. Closing lines should be about real/selective/stand-out, not price.

**Two audiences:** parent-facing copy uses "your child"; student-facing copy (Instagram) speaks directly to the student ("you"), peer / older-sibling tone, no parent-anxiety. Student-targeted variants live in [[matchtern-static-ad-pipeline]] (`student-*.png`).

**Target segment — business-bound students** (business / finance / marketing majors): Alex wants to reach business-minded students. Business-track creative exists (`ad-7-business`, `student-business`). Keep copy broad, not over-specific (Alex prefers not naming specific schools/numbers). Meta reality: you CANNOT interest-target under-18s (age + location only) — so reach this segment via parent audiences (interest-targetable: finance/investing/entrepreneurship/business), lookalikes of enrolled families, and business-angled creative that self-selects on broad/age-only delivery.
