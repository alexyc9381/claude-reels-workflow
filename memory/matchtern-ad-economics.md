Grounded 2026 benchmarks (Superads $3B dataset, LeadSync, AdAmigo; medium confidence, wide variance) for Matchtern's Meta lead-magnet funnel, cross-checked by an adversarial pass:

**Cost per email opt-in (US, niche parent audience, broad/Employment-SAC targeting, $50/day):** landing-page opt-in **~$40–65, plan ~$50, aim to beat ~$45**; Meta Instant Forms ~$25–45 but lower-intent. (Base US education opt-ins ~$30–42, adjusted up for niche + broad + sub-learning-phase budget. NOT $4–12 — an early wrong ballpark.)

**The real finding: opt-in cost is a minor lever.** Show rate + close rate swing the outcome 5–8x; opt-in price only ~30%. Realistic MIDPOINT funnel at $50/day (~$1,500/mo): $50 opt-in → 30 opt-ins → 7% book (~2 calls) → 60% show w/ reminders (~1.3) → 22% close → **~0.28 sales/mo ≈ 1 sale per 3–4 months, ~$5,300 cost per sale vs the $2,000 offer ≈ 0.4x ROAS. Money-LOSING** across the realistic middle. Breakeven needs 4 top-of-range assumptions to hit at once.

**Implication (STANDING for Matchtern ad strategy):** a $2k cold offer at $50/day is structurally underwater; a lead magnet lowers CPL + gives Meta enough signal to exit learning, but does NOT fix the economics. Levers that matter: (1) self-qualifying creative (business parents only), (2) show rate = the reminder/confirmation stack (see [[matchtern-apply-landing-page]] back-half), (3) close rate on the call. To reach profitability, likely need one of: raise budget (volume), or **add a lower-ticket tripwire** ($50–200 mini-product/workshop/assessment) between the free guide and the $2k call so cold traffic isn't asked to jump free→$2k. Judge the funnel on cost-per-showed-call + cost-per-sale, never cost-per-opt-in. Treat the first ~$1–2k as validation spend expected to lose money.
