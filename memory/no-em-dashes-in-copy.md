Alex wants **zero em dashes** (`—` / `&mdash;` / `&#8212;`) in Matchtern copy. He flagged it on the apply.matchtern.org site ("dont have any em dashes on the website whatsoever") and had me sweep all 4 pages to 0.

**Why:** the em dash is a well-known AI-writing/"vibecoded" tell, and Alex is strongly anti-AI-looking across his brand (see [[matchtern-apply-landing-page]] "less vibecoded" work). Treat this as applying to ALL copy I write for him — landing pages, FB/IG ad copy, reel scripts/captions, lead-magnet docs, emails — not just the website.

**How to apply:** never use `—` for a parenthetical or emphatic break. Restructure instead: split into two sentences (period), use a comma, a colon, or parentheses. Do a pre-ship grep for `&mdash;|—|&#8212;` on any HTML/copy I generate. En dashes in number ranges are fine; the target is the em dash used as punctuation. (Also avoid other AI tells Alex dislikes: overuse of "elevate/unlock/seamless," three-part rhythm lists, etc.)
