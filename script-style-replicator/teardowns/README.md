# teardowns/ — the forensic layer

> one-line stamp: per-video autopsies of each creator's biggest hits, dissected verbatim, line-by-line and word-by-word, into copyable fill-in-the-blank formulas.

## What this layer is

Each file in `teardowns/<name>.md` takes a creator's top ~4–5 videos **by actual view count** and dissects them the way a coroner works a body: the exact transcript, quoted verbatim, with every sentence tagged by structural component (hook / receipt / mechanism / re-hook / payoff / CTA), then a word-by-word gloss of *why each word is load-bearing*, then a runtime map, then a **fill-in-the-blank formula** you can pour a new topic into, then a rule-checked `COPY IT (Alex)` rebuild.

This is the deepest of three fidelity layers in this skill:

| Layer | File | Grain | Answers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Voice (aggregate)** | `creators/<name>-dna.md` | the creator's *whole style* averaged across their catalog — hook families, phrase bank, generation template | "what does a raycfu script *sound like* in general?" |
| **Opener (swipe file)** | `HOOK-BANK.md` | 41+ proven *first lines* only, tagged by family, ranked by views | "give me a proven opening line for this topic" |
| **Video (forensic)** | `teardowns/<name>.md` ← *you are here* | one *specific proven viral video*, reverse-engineered whole | "clone the exact structure of a hit that already worked" |

**When to use it:** when you want to CLONE a specific proven viral video's exact structure for a new topic. Not "write in mavgpt's voice" (that's the DNA), and not "give me a hook" (that's the bank) — but "rebuild ERASE (1.3M) beat-for-beat around *my* topic." You inherit the whole machine: the hook shape, the verbatim transitional phrases, the re-hook placement, the escalation order, and the CTA cut. The DNA tells you the grammar; the teardown hands you a sentence that already went viral and lets you swap the nouns.

## ⭐ THE UNIVERSAL VIRAL LAWS

Cross-referencing all 8 forensic files, these are the mechanics that recur in **nearly every hit, across every creator** — the load-bearing structure that transfers no matter the niche, the tone, or whether the video is a fear-thriller, a money-playbook, or a live awe-demo. If a new script violates one of these, it is fighting the data. Each law is stated as a rule, then the teardowns that prove it.

### Law 1 — The value noun lands by ~word 12
The thing the viewer wants (or fears) is named in the first breath. No throat-clearing, no "in this video," no channel intro. The first word is a payload word.
> **Proven in all 8.** mavgpt ("digital footprint" by word 9) · raycfu ("dev team" by word 11) · nateherk ("tool"/"websites" by word ~6) · nicksaraev ("websites" by word 6–8) · cindiezhu ("creative studio" by word 10) · gregisenberg ("Claude" by word 5, the loss-metric by word 8) · rileybrown ("booking/dating app," "Blender" at the impossible-pairing joint, ~word 11–12) · sabrinaramonov ("digital footprint" by word 9).

### Law 2 — Open one loop, then pay it before doubt forms
A curiosity gap (a question, a shock claim, or an awe interjection) opens in sentence one; a **receipt** closes it in sentence two — an instant proof beat that certifies the payoff *before* asking for any attention investment.
> **Proven in all 8.** mavgpt (Q-hook → "I tried this and it actually wiped my identity off hundreds of websites") · raycfu (secret-gap hook → the before→after) · nateherk (result-hook stated as accomplished fact → visual "boom" receipt) · nicksaraev (the drop → the running-cost/skeptic receipt) · cindiezhu ("Did you know… can now?" → social-receipt) · gregisenberg (loss-frame hook → the transformation payoff) · rileybrown (awe word → the plumbing/database reveal) · sabrinaramonov ("What happens when…?" → "it actually wiped my identity," *the single fastest loop→receipt in the corpus*).

### Law 3 — Name and number the mechanism
The engine is never "an AI" or "a file." It is a **named object with a count**: agent mode, `.pipeline.md`, the Scanner/the Surgeon, GWS, the taste skill, memory.md, four agents, seven skills, three steps. Naming makes it feel real, copy-pasteable, and repeatable-to-a-friend (which drives shares); numbering gives the brain a countable path (which drives retention to the end).
> **Proven in all 8.** raycfu (Planner/Coder/Tester/Reviewer — named characters) · cindiezhu (the diagnosis/the recruiter/the rewriter/the hiring manager — persona nouns) · gregisenberg (agents.md/context folder/memory.md/skills folder — named artifacts) · mavgpt (agent mode, Connectors, the 6-step click-path) · nateherk (GWS, Nano Banana, the "four things") · nicksaraev (the taste skill, "three steps," "300 sub-agents") · rileybrown (Opus 4.6, nano banana API, coined "vibecoded agentic application") · sabrinaramonov ("agent mode," "Phase one/two/three," "all four prompts").

### Law 4 — One concrete receipt beats every adjective
Specificity *is* the proof. A hard, un-roundable number, a before→after, or an on-screen artifact does the persuading in place of testimonials. Never "cheap" — always "36 cents." Never "fast" — always "3 days into 30 seconds." A figure lands roughly every 20–40 seconds.
> **Proven in all 8.** nicksaraev ("5.3 megs down to 252 kilobytes," "36 cents," the running cost meter) · gregisenberg ("10% of its brain," "4-hour task into a single instruction") · raycfu ("reduced churn by 18% over six months for 3,000 people") · nateherk ("50 million lines migrated in a single day," number in hook AND payoff) · sabrinaramonov ("$50 × 200 = $10K/mo," "hundreds of websites") · mavgpt ("hundreds of websites," "your address and phone number") · rileybrown ("one single prompt," "7 minutes and 26 seconds later," clicking into the live database) · cindiezhu (a hard number in *every* hook — "seven skills," "1,000 job descriptions," "$3,000").

### Law 5 — The viewer is the subject: second person, present tense
"You / your" saturates; the tool acts *now*, in front of *you*, in present or present-progressive tense. No "I recommend," no past-tense storytelling, no faceless third party. The demo is happening live and it is happening to the viewer.
> **Proven in all 8.** sabrinaramonov ("you/your" is the single most-used word) · nateherk ("you guys" 40+ times; "it's creating," "it's asking us") · cindiezhu ("your video," "your resume," "your room") · gregisenberg ("you/your" ~11× in 144 words) · nicksaraev ("you," "you could," "let me show you") · rileybrown ("This right here is," "you could go to this link right now") · mavgpt (every prompt object is "me/you," present-tense imperatives) · raycfu ("your resume," "you're missing," dense second person throughout).

### Law 6 — Zero jargon: one insider noun max, immediately glossed
The whole thing must pass a 12-year-old parse. At most one near-technical term per beat, and it is instantly re-said in plain words or an analogy ("agent mode… visit each site and submit the removal request"; "run rate basically means businesses are paying at a pace of $47B a year"; ".pipeline is a shared folder where the next agent reads it"). The "which means" / "basically like" translator is a recurring device.
> **Proven in all 8.** nicksaraev ("global CDN… which means people everywhere load your site fast" — the payoff-translator) · nateherk ("these are basically like skills") · cindiezhu ("multi-step agent" instantly re-said as "runs in the background while you work") · gregisenberg (every artifact gets a "like onboarding an employee" gloss) · sabrinaramonov ("agent mode" is the most technical noun in the top video, defined by function) · mavgpt (plain English + one sinister noun per beat) · raycfu (engineer nouns as texture, never load-bearing) · rileybrown (real dev nouns wrapped in slang — "database," "back end," delivered casually). *(Anti-pattern flagged in the files: nateherk's Fable 5 and nicksaraev's $80k Agency drift into FFmpeg/GSAP/"agent swarm/bypassing permissions" — the teardowns mark those as the exact spots Alex must dial down.)*

### Law 7 — Contractions in, filler out — the first word is payload
Spoken register only: "gonna," "here's," "it's," "you're." Zero "hey guys," zero "um, so basically," zero "in this video I'll show you." The formal, written-out word is a tell of an outsider. The scroll-stopper is word one.
> **Proven in all 8.** mavgpt (contractions in every payoff verb; first word is "What" or the capability) · raycfu ("the first word of every video is a payload word") · nicksaraev (contractions 100%; filler is ASR texture that dies in the written reel) · gregisenberg (zero em-dashes, zero filler intros across all transcripts) · rileybrown ("no 'hey guys,' no channel intro; the demo is already mid-run in sentence one") · nateherk (contractions near-total; filler-as-authenticity is deliberate, but never an intro) · cindiezhu (no throat-clearing; value noun by word 8) · sabrinaramonov (contractions everywhere, causal connectors open the beats).

### Law 8 — One fixed mid-video re-hook at the drop point
A short, pre-written, reused-verbatim phrase resets attention at the exact moment short-form viewers bail — usually right before the biggest payoff. It is a module, not an improvisation: the same creator drops the *same words* across multiple hits.
> **Proven in all 8.** mavgpt ("Now here's the trick." — verbatim in 4 of 7 videos) · sabrinaramonov ("Now here's the trick." AND "Here's the best part." — byte-for-byte identical across both TikToks) · raycfu ("This is the most important part." / "Now this is where the magic happens.") · gregisenberg ("But feeding context alone isn't enough." — the "But [X] alone isn't enough" pivot, 7+ times across the set) · cindiezhu ("OK so this is the fun part.") · nicksaraev (the skeptic-frame flip + the "deflate reprise" before the CTA) · rileybrown (the ITERATE turn: "but I do want to make a few changes.") · nateherk (the anaphora reveal "Not via X. Not via Y. But via Z." + "the trick is…").

### Law 9 — Escalate: save the biggest beat for last
Every beat is a bigger dopamine hit than the last, so attention never plateaus, and the single most powerful capability is parked near the end. Info → artifact → autonomous action → forever-loop; or step 1 (small) → final step (the climax), explicitly flagged as the biggest.
> **Proven in all 8.** mavgpt (find → write → autonomous submit → scheduled task that runs forever) · gregisenberg ("And lastly, create the skills folder…" — the longest sentence, the climax step, saved for last by rule) · raycfu (the differentiator spotlight parked near the end — the .pipeline secret, the skill-library moat) · nicksaraev (the scale-out: prove one, then "run this across 5, 10, 20 instances") · cindiezhu ("It's even gonna… it'll even…" escalation adverbs) · rileybrown (the escalation prompt — a bigger, vaguer, "use your imagination" ask as the wow-peak) · sabrinaramonov ("Here's the best part" compounding payoff + phase escalation) · nateherk (proof stacks that narrow to the most relevant capability; nested counting scaffolds).

### Law 10 — Gate the how, then cut hard on a one-word keyword
Show the *what* generously (rough steps, the plain-English command, the named modules) but withhold the exact copy-paste recipe (the full prompt, the file contents, the config). The withheld recipe is what the CTA sells: a single, lowercase, on-theme keyword — ideally echoing the title or the payoff verb — and the video **cuts on that word** with no trailing tail.
> **Proven natively in 6 of 8**, and the exception is instructive. mavgpt ("comment erase" — keyword = title) · sabrinaramonov ("comment erase" — keyword echoes the payoff verb; and her A/B twin proves *banking the ask at the END* is worth ~4x reach) · raycfu ("comment dev and I'll send it to you" — shows the architecture, withholds the prompts = a fair trade) · cindiezhu ("Comment skills" — the full install guide lives behind the word) · nateherk ("comment [KEYWORD] and it's yours" — gated depth = engineered curiosity) · rileybrown ("If you want to learn how to do this, comment ___" — cuts on the word, the cleanest model in the set). **The exception → the lesson:** nicksaraev and gregisenberg close *cold* (Nick gives prompts away free as a brand stance; Greg ends on a transformation line with no CTA). Their `COPY IT` rebuilds all bolt a keyword gate on — the natural withhold is already there (Nick never gives the skill's contents; Greg names the files but not the templates), so Alex only formalizes it.

### Law 11 — Open on an enemy the viewer already feels
Most hits weaponize a pre-existing emotion before offering the fix: a named villain ("a robot trashes your resume before a human sees it"), a loss-frame ("you pay for Claude and waste 90% of its brain"), a taboo fear ("your address is being sold"), or a cost you're about to avoid ("before you drop $3,000…"). The status quo is attacked so the payoff feels earned, not offered.
> **Proven in 7 of 8** — the one exception maps the boundary. raycfu ("most people" as the named enemy in every hook) · sabrinaramonov ("Most people start with the last step… that's why nothing works"; the privacy-horror taboo) · gregisenberg (the sunk-cost loss-frame: "10% of its brain," "feels like slop") · cindiezhu (the objection-preempt: "before you spend thousands of dollars and thousands of hours") · nateherk (universal pain: "you know how getting AI to write a Google Doc looks like horrible raw markdown?") · nicksaraev (skepticism-as-enemy + the complexity wall to collapse) · mavgpt (privacy horror / profession-death fear frames). **The exception:** rileybrown runs on pure **awe and novelty** ("Oh my god… this shouldn't be possible") rather than a loss-frame — proof that an impossible-pairing spectacle can substitute for an enemy when the demo itself is jaw-dropping enough. Use the enemy by default; earn the right to skip it only with a genuinely un-toppable live payoff.

**How to read the laws:** 1–7 are *diction/structure hygiene* — they should be true of literally every line you write. 8–11 are *retention architecture* — the beats you deliberately place. A script that passes all 11 is inheriting the measured consensus of 33 proven hits.

## How it plugs into the Script Factory

The teardowns feed the two middle stages of the pipeline. (See `SKILL.md` → "HOW TO PLUG INTO THE SCRIPT FACTORY.")

- **Stage 2 — structure (`structure_comp`):** instead of inheriting the creator's *aggregate* beat map (the DNA), point `structure_comp` at **one specific torn-down video** and inherit its exact runtime map and beat order. "Structure this like ERASE (1.3M)" or "clone the Dev-Team-4-Agents skeleton" gives the draft a proven, view-validated spine — not an averaged one. Use the file's **extracted fill-in-the-blank formula** as the literal scaffold and pour the topic into the slots.

- **Stage 3 — draft / diction (exact-phrase):** lift the video's **verbatim transitional phrases** ("Now here's the trick.", "This is the most important part.", "which means…", "Not via X. Not via Y. But via Z.") and its word-level moves (the totalizers, the payoff-translators, the receipt cadence) straight into the draft. This is higher-fidelity than the DNA phrase bank because it is the *actual load-bearing language of a single hit*, in its original order.

- **Pair with the FUSION PROTOCOL** (`CREATOR-MATRIX.md`). Every torn-down video is a raw creator artifact — it will contain first-person anecdote ("I tried this…"), an em-dash cadence, an ungated giveaway, or a trailing "see you over there" tail. The FUSION PROTOCOL is the transform layer that converts each of those into Alex-compliant form: first-person → third-person receipt · em-dash → comma/period · ungated → keyword gate · tail → hard cut on the keyword · jargon → plain gloss · value noun pulled to ~word 12. Every teardown file already demonstrates this on its own hits via the `COPY IT (Alex)` rebuilds — read those as worked examples of teardown-formula × FUSION PROTOCOL. The forensic layer gives you the proven machine; the protocol makes it Alex's.

**One-line flow:** *pick the hit → copy its fill-in-the-blank formula (Stage 2) + its verbatim phrases (Stage 3) → run the FUSION PROTOCOL → gate through the Stage-1/3/4 factory gates → ship.*
