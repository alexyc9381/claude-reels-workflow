## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON
```
1. HOOK          "Did you know [that] [Claude / you] can now [OUTCOME YOU WANT] [with just N skills/tools]?"
2. OBJECTION     "So before you [spend $X / waste hours / hire an agency], try this."
3. ITEM ×N       "Number [n], [PERSONA/NAME]. So this [does X], which means [OUTCOME you feel]."
   (or STEP ×N)  "OK so first, go to [tool], [click/paste/type] [thing], and then [next micro-action]."
4. FRICTION      "No editing needed." / "and that's literally it." / "you don't have to be a developer."
   RE-HOOK       "OK so this is the fun part."   (dropped right before the payoff)
5. RECAP / AWE   "So that's the whole loop. [verb], [verb], [verb], [verb]." / "It's insane."
6. CTA           "Comment [KEYWORD] … full setup guide for the [N]."
```

### The EXACT transitional phrases she reuses VERBATIM across videos (quote them)
These are her equivalents of mavgpt's "Now here's the trick." — the load-bearing seams, repeated word-for-word:

- **"Did you know that … can now …"** — the opener seam. 3 of the top 4 open on this literal string (292K, 25.6K, 12.3K, 7.5K = all 4 actually).
- **"So before you [X], try this."** / **"…do this first."** — the objection seam. 3 of top 4 (292K "So before you spend thousands of dollars and thousands of hours"; 12.3K "So before you repaint, rearrange, or drop $3,000… do this first"; 7.5K "So before you spend hours trying to figure out how… try this").
- **"which means …"** — the outcome-translator seam, dropped after a feature. Verbatim in 292K ("which means all their messaging, hooks, and CTAs that are working right now").
- **"Number one, … Number two, … Number three, …"** — the ordinal list seam (292K, 25.6K).
- **"So this skill …"** — the item-open seam (292K, 25.6K, both use "So this skill" to start item bodies).
- **"OK so first …"** / **"Okay first"** / **"Okay, so this is the fun part."** — the step seams (12.3K, 7.5K).
- **"and that's all"** / **"and that's literally it"** / **"and that's literally it."** — the step-is-trivial seam (12.3K "and that's all"; 7.5K "and that's literally it").
- **"It's insane."** — the awe seam (12.3K "Claude does everything. It's insane.").
- **"Comment [KEYWORD] … the full [setup/install] guide"** — the CTA seam (292K "Comment skills"; 25.6K "Comment resume"; 12.3K "comment banana").

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (specific words + word-classes, with counts across the top 4)
- **"now" / "can now"** — the novelty flag. Appears in the hook of all 4 (292K, 25.6K, 12.3K, 7.5K), plus body uses ("working right now"). ~6 occurrences. This single adverb does the outlier work: it reframes a known tool as newly capable.
- **"literally"** — her #1 intensifier. Counted across top 4: "with just seven skills" context, 7.5K alone has "People are literally generating" + "and that's literally it" + "literally takes a few minutes" (3 in one 199-word script); 292K has none but her corpus leans on it. ~5+ across the four.
- **Hard numbers in every hook, zero exceptions:** "seven skills" (292K), "four skills" (25.6K), "$3,000" (12.3K), "1,000 similar job descriptions" (25.6K), "a month worth" (7.5K), "out of 10" (25.6K). Rule: never "some" or "a few" in the hook.
- **Second person "you / your"** saturates: "your video," "your resume," "your room," "your carousel topic," "you don't have to." She never says "one" or "people" where "you" fits. (First person "I" only appears in her non-top videos; the top 4 are almost pure "you," which is why they translate cleanly to Alex.)
- **Present tense, active:** "it adds animations," "this skill pulls," "Claude generates," "it becomes," "Claude does everything." No past-tense storytelling in the top 4.
- **Contractions everywhere:** "you can," "that's," "it's," "don't," "you're," "there's," "no one's." Zero formal register.
- **Persona nouns with a definite article:** "the diagnosis, the recruiter, the rewriter, the hiring manager" (25.6K) — turns a feature into a character with a job.
- **Sensory / decisive verbs on the mechanism:** "pulls," "scans," "flags," "rewrites," "grabs," "shrink," "drop," "generates," "builds," "deploys." Short, physical, no abstraction.
- **Deliberately LEFT OUT:** jargon (no "subagent," "orchestration," "vector," "API-parsing" in the sold lines — "API" appears once, buried in a step, never in a hook), hedges ("maybe," "kind of"), and setup throat-clearing. She hits the value noun by ~word 8–12 every time.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS she pulls every single time (ranked by force)
1. **Open loop via novelty question** — "Did you know … can now …?" You cannot mute-scroll past a question that implies you're missing a new capability.
2. **Specificity as proof** — a hard number in the hook ("seven skills," "$10,000") makes the claim feel real and finite, not hype.
3. **Objection-preempt = stake + save** — "before you spend thousands, try this" installs a cost you're about to avoid; the free method now feels like rescued money.
4. **Novelty of the capability itself** — each video is a "Claude can now do a thing it obviously couldn't before" reveal (interior designer, ATS-beater, whole studio).
5. **Social proof receipt** — "People are literally generating a month of carousels in minutes" / "agencies generate hundreds of UGC ads while they sleep" — third-person, so it reads as trend not brag.
6. **One-word comment gate** — converts curiosity into the algorithm's favorite signal while withholding the real work.
