## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON (fires in all 4, in this exact order)
1. **SECRET-GAP HOOK** — one sentence, a number inside it, the value noun by ~word 10, the enemy is "most people."
2. **PROMISE-BRIDGE** — a 5-to-7-word sentence that restates the payoff and counts the steps ("This is how you set it up.").
3. **(optional) WRONG-WAY / LOSS FRAME** — what most people do, and what it costs them, so the right way feels earned.
4. **NAMED STEPS ×3–5** — each is a noun-phrase LABEL spoken as its own fragment ("The Scanner."), then *what it does* → *why it matters* → *a concrete number or before→after*.
5. **DIFFERENTIATOR SPOTLIGHT** — the single deepest step, flagged as the thing that separates this from everything else, parked near the end.
6. **GATED CTA** — "I created a detailed guide… comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it to you."
7. **FOLLOW-TAIL** — "Follow for more advice on making money and running your business with tech and AI." (**Alex deletes this; it violates cut-on-keyword.**)

### The EXACT transitional phrases reused VERBATIM across videos
These are load-bearing connective tissue; he does not paraphrase them, he re-fires them:
- **Hook triggers:** "Most people don't realize that…" (3 of 4 hits: Dev Team, Resume, Skills) · "Nobody is talking about this, but…" ($80k Agency).
- **Promise-bridge:** "This is how you set it up." (Dev Team) · "So, where do you start?" ($80k Agency) · "Here's what you actually need to build this out." ($80k Agency).
- **Differentiator spotlight (his single most reused retention line):** "This is the most important part." (Dev Team, verbatim) · "Now this is where the magic happens." ($80k Agency, verbatim). Across his wider catalog the same slot reads "This final part is the one that separates this… from everything else."
- **Category headers:** "The niche." "The tech stack." "The client acquisition." ($80k Agency) · "The scanner." "The Surgeon." "The Stress Test." "The sparring partner." (Resume) · "For marketing," "Design." "For developers," "For everyone," (Skills).
- **CTA stem (near word-for-word every time):** "I created a detailed guide… just like the video and comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it to you."
- **Follow-tail stem:** "Follow for more advice on making money and running your business with tech and AI." (Dev Team, $80k, Resume) / "Follow for more tech and AI." (Skills, trimmed).

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (the words + word-classes he leans on EVERY time, counted across the 4 hits)
- **"Most people" as the named enemy — 4 occurrences of the exact phrase in hook position** (Dev Team, Resume, Skills open on it; it recurs mid-body in others). The whole voice is built on positioning you *against* "most people."
- **"realize / don't realize" — 3 hooks** (Dev Team, Resume, Skills). The promised feeling is *becoming an insider*.
- **Second person "you / your" — dense and constant:** Resume ≈ 20+ instances ("your resume," "you are applying for," "shows you everything," "you already have a sense"), Skills ("that you can import," "your actual cost," "your files"), $80k ("you keep 90%," "you can sell it," "you connect it"). He is always talking *at* you, never *about* himself.
- **Present tense, active voice, throughout.** "The coder agent reads the spec and builds exactly what it says." "It scans your resume for specific keywords." "KIMI is also 80% cheaper." No past-tense storytelling except the closing "I created a guide."
- **Numbers / dollar figures — a number roughly every 2–3 sentences, always concrete, never rounded-to-vibes:** 4 agents, Opus 4, Sonnet 4.6, $80k/month, $50,000/project, 90%, $5,000–$7,000, 80%, 300 sub-agents, 80% cheaper, 18% over six months, 3,000 people, 200 resumes, 9 or 10, 60,000 skills, 100+, 277,000 installs, 96,000 stars. Numbers do the persuading in place of testimonials.
- **Sensory / "violent" outcome verbs:** "ship out features while you are asleep," "gets sent to the trash," "bleeding money" (loss-frame from his catalog), "unrejectable," "escapes the generic AI look," "no competitor can compete with." Verbs are physical, not abstract.
- **Causal connectors are the skeleton — because / so / so that / instead / no matter:** "Use Opus 4… **because** the quality of a plan sets the ceiling," "writes to a shared folder… **so that** every agent has context," "**Instead of** 'manage the team' it becomes…," "gets sent to the trash **no matter how** qualified you are." Every step earns a *why*. Remove the causal clause and the voice dies.
- **Contractions everywhere** ("don't," "here's," "you're," "can't," "I'll," "it's") — keeps it spoken, not written. **Filler removed:** zero "hey guys," zero "um," zero "so basically." The first word of every video is a payload word.
- **Named-tool specificity as a credibility tic:** Opus 4, Sonnet, Kimi K2, MCP (GitHub/Postgres/Supabase/Slack), Corey Haines, ATS, Google XYZ formula, Deloitte, Google, Meta, Fortune 500. Proper nouns = "this is real."

### The VIRALITY LEVERS pulled every single time
1. **Open loop / curiosity gap** — "most people don't realize" promises a secret and withholds it for the length of the video.
2. **Insider status / in-group flattery** — cross this line and you're ahead of "most people." Cheapest, most reusable lever he owns.
3. **Specificity as proof** — exact model names, exact percentages, exact install counts. Specific = true-feeling.
4. **Stakes / loss-frame** — "gets sent to the trash no matter how qualified you are," "two weeks later they are bleeding money."
5. **Named-object memorability** — "The Scanner / The Surgeon" turns a workflow into a set of characters you can picture and repeat, which drives shares and saves.
6. **The gate as fair trade** — he genuinely shows the architecture and withholds only the copy-paste prompts, so the comment CTA reads as earned, not bait.
