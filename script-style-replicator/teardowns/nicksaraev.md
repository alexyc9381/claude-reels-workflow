## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON
Every one of his top videos runs the same seven-beat spine. Compressed for a reel it is six beats.

1. **THE DROP** — a "just happened" event names the value noun in the first breath. (`[X] just [dropped/launched/built] [VALUE NOUN], which [outcome].`)
2. **THE SKEPTIC FRAME** — undercut the hype in the same breath so you become the credible narrator. (`I initially thought this was just going to be some hype BS` / `anytime somebody tells me they oneshot stuff, I'm inherently skeptical`)
3. **THE CREDENTIAL / RECEIPT** — a former-agency authority beat welded to a hard dollar number. (`this would probably be 5 to 10,000 dollars a pop and I know that because I used to sell websites for a living`)
4. **THE DEFLATE** — show the scary "everyone overcomplicates this" wall, then collapse it to "one prompt / three steps." (`it's way simpler than everybody else is making it out to be... There's really just three steps`)
5. **THE LIVE DEMO / RECEIPTS** — narrate the build, perform surprise, drop the running cost meter. (`that's what, three or four bucks, realistically?` / `it just compressed it from 5.3 megs down to 252 kilobytes`)
6. **THE SCALE-OUT** — prove one case, then 10–20x the vision. (`You could parallelize this, run this across five or 10 or 20 instances`)
7. **THE CTA** — free prompt/repo below + the 73%-not-subscribed guilt lever.

### The EXACT transitional phrases he reuses VERBATIM across videos
These are the load-bearing connective tissue. Quote-for-quote reuse, cross-video:

- **"to make a long story short"** — the compression valve, appears in 5 of 7 transcripts ("to make a long story short, it is nowhere near that complicated" / "to make a long story short, one of the former founding members of OpenAI... is called Andrej Karpathy").
- **"in a nutshell"** — same job, opening a build ("So, in a nutshell, our flow is going to be as follows.").
- **"There's really just three steps"** / **"three simple steps"** / **"you need three ingredients"** — the rule-of-three deflate, in all four demo videos.
- **"The reason why... is because..."** / **"the reason I want to make this video is because"** — his causal hinge, everywhere.
- **"which means..."** / **"so what that means is..."** — the payoff-translator after any technical noun ("global CDN... which means people in all different parts of the world will be able to access your website quickly").
- **"And here's why."** — the open-loop stinger (Clawdbot: "Claudebot sucks actually. And here's why.").
- **"Anyway,"** — his reset button, returning from every tangent (Clawdbot uses "Anyway," 6+ times as a hard scene-cut).
- **"So, hopefully you guys [see / appreciated] ..."** — the CTA on-ramp, in all four.
- **"Now,"** / **"So,"** / **"Okay,"** — sentence-initial 3-of-4 sentences in demo sections; the verbal cursor-move.

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (specific words + word-classes, with counts across the 4 top videos)

- **"just" as a freshness/minimizer particle** — his single most-loaded word. Two jobs: (a) recency in the hook ("I **just** built these four websites," "Anthropic **just** launched routines"), (b) minimizing effort in the demo ("I literally **just** one-shotted it," "**just** make it faster," "it's **just** a prompt"). - **"literally"** — the reality-anchor intensifier, ~15+ uses ("you can **literally** do it in less than 10 minutes," "I **literally** just said make it faster," "it **literally** becomes self-improving AI").
- **"clean" as the #1 adjective** — the aesthetic verdict word. In the two website videos alone: "really really clean," "super clean," "way higher quality," "pretty clean. Pretty clean," "so much cleaner," "Damn, I think that looks clean," "That is clean. That is really clean." 20+ uses.
- **Violent / physical verbs for software** — "**destroys** every site designer," "**kills** n8n," "**crush**," "**blows my mind,**" "**chews** through tokens," "spits out," "shove that into Slack," "rug pull," "pump and dump." Software is given a body and made to hit things.
- **Sensory / lust adjectives** — "sexy" (scroll effects, sites), "gorgeous," "unreal," "insane," "wild," "maximalist," "immersive." He sexualizes clean design deliberately.
- **Second person, relentless** — "you," "you guys," "you could," "let me show **you**." Nearly every sentence in a demo is addressed. "you guys" ~50+ across the 4.
- **Present / imperative tense in the build** — "Generate me a...," "Make the background white," "make it faster," "mobile optimize the site." The prompts he pastes are all barked imperatives; the narration around them is present continuous ("it's going to fetch," "now it's adding scroll reveal logic").
- **Numbers, always specific, never vague** — dollar figures ("$15K," "5 to 10,000 dollars a pop," "36 cents," "$5 in tokens," "$300," "16 mil," "$2.40," "$204"), file sizes ("5.3 megs down to 252 kilobytes"), percentages ("73%," "90% token crash"), counts ("three steps," "four websites," "900 Cloudbot instances," "16-year-old"). - **Filler he uses (spoken) and Alex must strip** — "like," "you know," "sort of," "kind of," "um," "and so on and so forth," "whatever the heck," "for whatever reason." These are ASR texture; the cadence survives, the filler dies in the written reel.
- **Doubled short beat (repeat-for-emphasis)** — "Pretty clean. Pretty clean." / "That is clean. That is really clean." / "Yeah. Yeah." The second utterance = the retention spike.
- **Rule of three** — "three steps," "three ingredients," "three simple steps," "spacing, high-end luxury looks and so on"; the triad is his default list length.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS he pulls every single time (ranked by recurrence)
1. **Freshness / open loop ("just dropped")** — pegs the video to a live wave; the viewer feels early. Present in all 4 hooks in some form.
2. **Skepticism-as-credibility (taboo against hype)** — he attacks the hype cycle he's part of, which buys trust for the one claim he wants believed. All 4.
3. **Complexity-collapse (the satisfaction lever)** — a scary wall ("35,000 steps," "17-step framework," n8n spaghetti) collapsed to "one prompt." The contrast IS the dopamine. All 4.
4. **Specificity / receipts** — hard numbers make outcomes feel real and repeatable. All 4, high density.
5. **Social proof + scale-dream** — a 16-year-old on Twitter, Karpathy, "$1M/y agency," then "now imagine this 20x." Borrowed authority + self-extrapolated money. 3 of 4.
6. **Novelty of the deliverable** — "$15K animated sites," "self-improving AI," "kills n8n." The noun itself is the promise. All 4.
