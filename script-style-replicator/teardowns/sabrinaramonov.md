### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (specific words + word-classes, with counts across the 4 videos)
- **Second person "you / your" — the dominant word.** She talks AT the viewer, never around them. 12 Ways alone: "your first dollar," "you get your first money," "you're selling your time," "you can package," dozens more. Both TikToks open on "you" ("when you ask," "you want to").
- **The soft command "you want to."** Used as a gentle imperative, 3× per TikTok: "you want to open up chat," "You want to hit the plus," "You want all four of these prompts." It commands without bossing.
- **Present tense + "just" for immediacy.** "Claude just dropped," "ChatGPT just," "I just sit in the cave." "Just" = 20+ instances across the four ("just tell the client," "just want the outcome," "just get 200 people," "just sit there").
- **The intensifier "literally."** Erase TikTok: "it literally found sites I've never heard of." 12 Ways: "literally refer to anything," "literally your time," "literally bring in a million dollars overnight," "literally tell you." A verbal underline on the claim.
- **Triple-stacking for emphasis.** "the hardest, hardest, hardest part" (AI Millionaire), "billions and billions and billions" (12 Ways), "many, many, many ideas" (AI Millionaire), "200 people. 200 people paying $50 per month" (repeated for drumbeat).
- **"Most people" as the villain.** The recurring foil. "Most people start with the last step," "Most people jump straight to phase three," "most people don't know," "99% of the time, people fail." The viewer is invited to not be them.
- **Numbers and dollar figures as texture.** At least one $ or hour figure every ~30s. $50,000/mo, $3,000 next week, $1,200, $15,000, $29/mo, $50×200=$10K/mo, 5–20x, $1–20M, "save 10 hours per week," "20 hours per week," "hundreds of websites," "all four of these prompts."
- **Causal connectors opening sentences.** "So first," "So that's," "But first," "And here's the best part," "The reason why is," "because." Beats OPEN with So / But / And / Now.
- **Contractions everywhere (spoken register).** "you're," "it's," "I'll," "nobody's," "you've," "don't." Zero stiff formality.
- **Sensory / violent verbs on the payoff.** "erase," "wiped," "delete," "disappears," "wipe my identity," "light a fire under your butt," "kill everything else," "crush that zero to 10,000." The action words are physical and final.
- **Deliberately LEFT OUT:** jargon. No "orchestration," no "pipeline," no "LLM." The most technical noun in the top video is "agent mode," immediately explained by what it does ("visit each site and submit the removal request"). Everything is 12-year-old parseable.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS pulled every single time (ranked by recurrence)
1. **Open loop by ~word 10.** A question or shock the brain needs closed ("What happens when you…?", "Claude just dropped something insane…").
2. **Instant receipt.** The claim is proven in the very next sentence, before doubt forms ("it actually wiped my identity off hundreds of websites").
3. **Specificity as proof.** Exact numbers, exact steps, exact keywords. "hundreds of websites," "all four of these prompts," "$50 × 200 = $10K."
4. **Novelty / secret framing.** "nobody knows about it," "most people don't know," "just dropped." The viewer is being let in early.
5. **Taboo / mild danger.** "erase your entire digital footprint," "terrifying," "sites I've never heard of." A little forbidden thrill.
6. **Outcome = time or money, never the tool.** Every hook resolves to hours saved or dollars made, so a non-technical viewer wants it without understanding it.
7. **Social proof (third-person receipts).** "a mom with…three kids who built…$50,000 per month," "I get DMs every single day."
8. **The gate = the comment engine.** Real value shown free, full asset behind a one-word comment → forces the algorithm signal.
