## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON
1. **AWE-INTERJECTION HOOK** — a 2-3 word reaction word, then a capability claim that bolts a normal tool to an impossible controller, value noun by ~word 12.
2. **SPOKEN LIVE PROMPT** — he reads the ask out loud as he types it (shows the how *partially*).
3. **WAIT → REACT** — a micro-cliffhanger ("let's see") paid off seconds later by a reaction word ("Boom", "Oh, wow").
4. **PROOF ASIDE** — he clicks into the plumbing (database, back end) to prove it's real, not a mockup.
5. **ITERATE TURN** — "but I do want to make a few changes" resets the tension after the first win.
6. **ESCALATION PROMPT** — a bigger, vaguer, more open-ended ask (the wow-peak).
7. **SHIP / PHYSICAL PAYOFF** — deploy to a public link, open it on a phone, submit to the app store. Something you can touch.
8. **CTA HANDOFF** — comment-keyword or "I made a longer video" route to the gated full method.

### The EXACT transitional phrases he reuses VERBATIM across videos (quote them)
- **"Oh my god."** / **"No way."** — the interchangeable cold-open awe word (Blender 704K; OpenClaw 154K/116K; the 1M's title is literally "Oh my.. this shouldn't be possible").
- **"This right here is my [TOOL] ... and it can literally control [X]."** — verbatim-identical opener across OpenClaw 154K and Blender pt2 116K (his reposted skeleton).
- **"All right, let's see."** — the pre-wait cliffhanger, before every generation resolves (hinge 1M ×2; OpenClaw).
- **"Okay, so it's done."** / **"Okay, it's done."** — the resolve marker that pays off the wait (hinge; video editor; OpenClaw).
- **"Boom."** / **"bang."** — the instant-result punctuation on a payoff (hinge ×3; MCP; OpenClaw "bang, there's Billy Bob").
- **"Let's go!"** / **"Oh, let's go!"** — the payoff exhale, stacked when big ("Oh, oh, let's go!") (Blender; Codex; OpenClaw).
- **"but I do want to make a few changes."** / **"here's my final prompt."** — the verbatim ITERATE-turn re-hook (hinge 1M; OpenClaw 154K).
- **"Please deploy this to Vercel. Send me a public link."** — verbatim ship line (OpenClaw 154K/116K).
- **"This is actually on the internet. You could go to this link right here."** — verbatim payoff-proof (OpenClaw ×2).
- **"Look at this."** / **"Look at that."** — the "point your eyes here" attention-yank (Blender; Codex; hinge).
- **"If you want to learn how to do this, comment ..."** — the cut-hard keyword CTA (MCP 183K).

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (specific words + word-classes he leans on EVERY time, with counts across the 4 teardown videos + the wider set)
- **Awe interjections:** "Oh my god" / "No way" / "Oh my.." — open 4 of 7 videos; "Oh, wow", "Ooh", "What the hell", "What is going on?" pepper the middles.
- **The intensifier "literally"** — 3 uses, all inside the "it can literally control Blender" opener; the signature hook-amplifier.
- **The intensifier "actually"** — "Codex is actually insane," "this is actually on the internet," "we can actually see," "AI is actually gonna create" — his second-favorite amp, ~9× across the set. Reframes a claim as verified-live.
- **"Boom" (5×) + "bang" (2×)** — onomatopoeic result-punctuation; a sound-effect made of a word.
- **"Let's go" (14×)** — the dopamine exhale; the single most repeated payoff phrase.
- **"Okay" (23×)** — the scene-cut / new-beat marker; the metronome of the whole format.
- **"Let's see" (9×)** — opens the wait, loads the cliffhanger.
- **"so" (37×) + "and so" (6×)** — his default connective glue; he chains observation to observation with "so" instead of stopping.
- **"please" (16×)** — almost all inside spoken prompts; the imperative verb that carries every build.
- **Second person + present tense** — "This right here is," "is now my," "can now control," "you could go to this link" — the demo is happening *now*, in front of *you*.
- **Number receipts (spiky, ~1 per 60-80 words):** "one single prompt," "two or three prompts," "100% ... in just three prompts," "7 minutes and 26 seconds later," "about 5 minutes." Numbers cluster only at proof beats.
- **Contractions everywhere** — "we're," "it's," "let's," "gonna," "I'm" — zero formal register; sounds like a friend narrating over your shoulder.
- **Deliberately LEFT OUT:** no "hey guys," no channel intro, no "in this video I'll show you," no throat-clearing setup, no adjectives-for-adjectives'-sake. The demo is already mid-run in sentence one.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS he pulls every single time
1. **Open loop (awe-before-explanation):** the reaction word fires curiosity before the brain has parsed *what* is surprising. You feel it, then you need to know why.
2. **Impossible pairing (novelty):** a normal tool welded to an unexpected controller (AI agent → Blender, Claude Code → video editor, vibe-coded app → your Slack). The gap IS the hook.
3. **Specificity / receipts:** exact model names, exact prompt counts, exact stopwatch times — un-roundable numbers that read as true.
4. **Un-fakeable real-time (social proof of self):** live narration with the fumbles left in, then the URL opened on his actual phone. Proof, not marketing.
5. **Escalation loop (retention):** win → "but change this" → bigger win, resetting tension so the curve never flattens.
6. **Physical payoff (stake resolved):** it always ends on something touchable and shareable — a live link, an app in the store.
