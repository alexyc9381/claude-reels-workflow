## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON
Every follow-along hit (ERASE, PHOTO, IDENTITY — the top 3) is the SAME machine, in this exact order:

1. **Q-Hook** — `What happens when you ask [AI] to [scary-or-magic OUTCOME named in full]?`
2. **Receipt** — `I tried this and it [actually/literally] [visceral result] [while I slept / photos I didn't even know existed].`
3. **Prompt 1 (open + say)** — `So first, [open/come over to] [AI] and say, [full prompt].`
4. **What-it-does narration** — `[AI] is then gonna [build/write/find] [every single / a full / custom] [artifact].`
5. **Shock beat** — `The results were actually terrifying.` (3–6 words, hard stop, standalone)
6. **Continuity + Prompt 2** — `Now [stay in the same chat] and say, [full prompt].`
7. **Trick flag + Prompt 3 (the insider UI move)** — `Now here's the trick.` + `[literal click-path]` + `and say, [full prompt].`
8. **Delegation payoff** — `[AI] is then gonna open its own browser and [do it] while you just sit there.`
9. **Bonus / best-part** — `And for the bonus prompt, say, [recurring-loop prompt].` → `[AI] builds you a scheduled task that runs every week until [end-state].`
10. **CTA** — `So if you want all these prompts and more, follow me and comment [KEYWORD].`

### The EXACT transitional phrases reused VERBATIM across videos (these are load-bearing — quote them)
- **"So first,"** — opens Prompt 1 in ERASE, PHOTO, IDENTITY, HUMAN. The starting gun.
- **"Now here's the trick."** — the single strongest mid-video re-hook. Appears VERBATIM in ERASE ("Now, here's the trick."), PHOTO ("Now here's the trick."), IDENTITY ("Now here's the trick."), HUMAN ("Now here's the trick."). 4 of the 7 videos. Flags an insider UI move.
- **"stay in the same chat"** — the continuity glue, VERBATIM in ERASE ("stay in the same chat and say"), IDENTITY ("stay in the same chat and say"), HUMAN ("Stay in the same chat").
- **"while I just sit there" / "while you just sit there"** — the delegation payoff, VERBATIM in ERASE ("while I just sit there") and IDENTITY ("while you just sit there").
- **"Here's the best part."** (ERASE) / **"And for the bonus prompt, say,"** (IDENTITY) / **"and now here's the bonus"** (PHOTO) — the ~80%-mark over-deliver flag.
- **"is then gonna"** — the what-it-does verb frame, VERBATIM across ERASE, PHOTO ("ChatGPT is then going to"), IDENTITY, HUMAN. The AI is always the grammatical subject that "is then gonna" do the work.
- **"So if you want all these prompts and more, make sure you follow me and comment [KEYWORD] and I'll shoot them over to you."** — the CTA, near-verbatim in PHOTO, IDENTITY, ERASE.
- **"The results were actually terrifying." / "The results were terrifying."** — the shock beat, VERBATIM in PHOTO, IDENTITY, ERASE.

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (the words he leans on EVERY time, with counts across the 7 transcripts)
- **"every single"** — the signature totalizer. PHOTO ×3 ("every specific feature"→"every single place"→"every single photo"), IDENTITY ×2, HUMAN ×2, ERASE ×1. Plus its siblings "every one of those," "each one," "every site," "hundreds of." Quantified vagueness is the whole numeric strategy.
- **"actually"** — the reality-stamp. PHOTO ×2, IDENTITY ×2, ERASE ×1, HUMAN ×1. Always attached to the scary/magic result ("actually terrifying," "actually wiped my identity," "actually passes every AI detector").
- **"literally"** — the hyperbole-stamp, twinned with "actually." PHOTO ("literally found me"), COLORS ("literally breaking the internet"), HUMAN ("literally came back as 0%"), DESIGN ("literally built the entire thing," "literally charging thousands"). 5 of 7 videos.
- **Second-person "you / your / yourself"** — the viewer is ALWAYS the subject. "every photo **of you**," "**your** entire digital footprint," "**your** face," "wash **you** out." Present-tense direct address, no "I recommend" softening.
- **Present-tense imperative verbs** — the follow-along spine, ~50%+ of content sentences: **open, say, upload, list, analyze, hit (the plus), turn on, go, stay, choose, install, recheck, resubmit, follow, comment.** Nearly every prompt beat is imperative-led.
- **Sensory/violent verbs** — chosen for gasp: **"erase," "wiped," "killed," "breaking," "terrifying," "brutal," "drain," "taken down," "wiped out for good."** Utility framed as a heist/horror.
- **Contractions everywhere** — "gonna" (never "going to" in the payoff beats), "wanna," "you're gonna," "it's," "I'll." Spoken-register only; zero formal syntax.
- **Filler removed** — no "hey guys," no "in this video," no "so basically." First word is "What" or the capability itself; the value noun lands by ~word 7–9.
- **Present-tense newsiness** — "can now," "just killed," "is literally breaking the internet." The capability is always brand-new *right now*.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS pulled every single time (ranked by force)
1. **Open loop with a PERSONAL stake** — the payoff is always about the viewer's own face/data/status/money, so the curiosity gap is impossible to leave.
2. **Taboo / fear frame** — privacy horror ("terrifying," data brokers "selling your address"), profession-death ("killed graphic designers"). Forbidden-knowledge pull.
3. **Perceived doability (follow-along)** — spoken imperatives over a live screen make the viewer feel they could do it right now, which drives the *save* (they save to try it) — the highest-signal engagement.
4. **Escalation ladder** — info → artifact → autonomous action → forever-loop. Every prompt is a bigger dopamine hit than the last, so attention never plateaus.
5. **Novelty / news peg** — a brand-new feature named exactly (agent mode, Connectors, Claude Design, skill creator).
6. **Social proof as credibility** — "breaking the internet," "people cannot stop posting their results."
7. **One-word keyword gate** — converts curiosity into a comment + a follow in the same breath.

## Teardown 1 — ERASE (1.3M views, lift ~3.3x median)

**Why it broke out:** it weaponizes the single most universal secret fear (my private data is being sold) and then shows an AI erasing it *for you while you sit there* — horror hook, heist payoff, zero-effort fantasy, all in one loop.

**Runtime map:**
- Hook (Q + receipt): words 0–28 (~0–8s)
- Prompt 1 + narration: words 29–66 (~8–18s)
- Shock beat: word ~67 (~18s)
- Prompt 2 + narration: words 68–95 (~18–26s)
- Trick + Prompt 3 + delegation payoff: words 96–140 (~26–38s)
- Best-part bonus + scheduled-task payoff: words 141–185 (~38–52s)
- CTA: words 186–210 (~52–58s)

**Annotated script — VERBATIM, line by line:**

> "What happens when you ask ChatGPT to erase your entire digital footprint off the internet?"
- **Component:** hook (open loop)
- **Does:** poses an irresistible what-if about the viewer's own exposure; the question format forces the brain to want the answer.
- **Word-level:** "**What happens when**" is a pure curiosity-gap frame (outcome withheld, promised). "**you ask**" = second person, viewer is the agent. "**erase**" is a violent, total verb (not "reduce" or "manage"). "**your entire digital footprint**" — "your" makes it personal, "entire" is the totalizer, "digital footprint" is the plain-English value noun landing by word 9. "**off the internet**" adds finality and scale. 16 words, ends on a hard question mark = the scroll-stopper.

> "I tried this and it actually wiped my identity off hundreds of websites."
- **Component:** receipt (closes the loop-promise, sets stakes)
- **Does:** proves the loop pays off before asking for any attention investment; "hundreds" sets the scale of the reward.
- **Word-level:** "**I tried this**" = first-person credibility (⚠️ Alex-rule collision, see COPY IT). "**actually**" = reality-stamp against disbelief. "**wiped**" = the violent totalizing verb echoing "erase." "**my identity**" raises the abstraction from "footprint" to "identity" (bigger stakes). "**hundreds of websites**" = quantified vagueness, the number-drop that isn't a real metric.

> "So first, when I open up ChatGPT and say search the internet for everything you can find about me."
- **Component:** mechanism-1 (Prompt 1)
- **Does:** starts the doable, click-by-click walkthrough; hands over step 1 as a literal spoken prompt.
- **Word-level:** "**So first,**" = the starting-gun signpost. "**open up**" and "**say**" = imperatives that make it feel executable. "**everything you can find about me**" = totalizer again ("everything"), first person as the prompt's object.

> "List every data broker and people search site that's exposing my personal info and giving me the exact opt-out link for each one."
- **Component:** mechanism-1 (Prompt 1 continued)
- **Does:** specifies the artifact precisely so it feels real and complete.
- **Word-level:** "**List**" = imperative. "**every data broker and people search site**" — "every" totalizer + specific villain nouns ("data broker") that sound sinister. "**exposing my personal info**" = the fear verb "exposing." "**the exact opt-out link for each one**" — "exact" and "each one" = specificity + totalizer stacked; "opt-out link" is the concrete artifact.

> "The results were terrifying."
- **Component:** retention-spike (shock beat)
- **Does:** resets attention with a 4-word hard stop right after a 24-word prompt sentence; the long-short cadence jolt.
- **Word-level:** "**The results**" = payoff noun. "**terrifying**" = the peak fear-word, standalone for maximum weight. Four words. No "were kind of." Absolute.

> "It literally found sites I've never heard of selling my address and phone number."
- **Component:** receipt (proof detail)
- **Does:** makes the horror concrete and personal — named data points the viewer also has.
- **Word-level:** "**literally**" = hyperbole-stamp. "**sites I've never heard of**" = the uncanny, out-of-control detail. "**selling my address and phone number**" — "selling" is the violation verb; "address and phone number" are the specific, universally-owned data that makes every viewer feel targeted.

> "Now, stay in the same chat and say write me the exact removal request to send to each one."
- **Component:** mechanism-2 (continuity + Prompt 2)
- **Does:** keeps the viewer in the flow and escalates from "find it" to "write the fix."
- **Word-level:** "**Now,**" = sequence signpost. "**stay in the same chat**" = the continuity glue (reused verbatim across videos). "**write me the exact removal request**" — escalation from information to artifact; "exact" specificity again. "**each one**" = totalizer.

> "ChatGPT is then gonna write out custom removal requests for each site."
- **Component:** what-it-does narration
- **Does:** shows the payoff of prompt 2 — the AI produced real deliverables.
- **Word-level:** "**ChatGPT is then gonna**" = the AI-as-subject verb frame (verbatim across videos). "**custom**" = personalization word (not generic). "**for each site**" = totalizer, scale of output.

> "Now, here's the trick."
- **Component:** retention-spike (trick flag)
- **Does:** the strongest mid-video re-hook; promises an insider move the viewer couldn't find alone. 4 words, hard stop.
- **Word-level:** "**here's the trick**" = forbidden-knowledge framing; "trick" implies a shortcut/cheat. The comma-pause "Now," delays and emphasizes.

> "You wanna hit the plus turn on agent mode and then say go to every one of those sites and submit the removal request for me."
- **Component:** mechanism-3 (Prompt 3, the escalation)
- **Does:** escalates to autonomous action — the AI now *does the task itself*, the biggest dopamine hit.
- **Word-level:** "**hit the plus**," "**turn on agent mode**" = literal UI clicks = doability. "**go to every one of those sites and submit the removal request for me**" — "every one" totalizer; "for me" = delegation; imperative chain "go... submit."

> "Now, ChatGPT is gonna open up its own browser and submit the entire list while I just sit there."
- **Component:** delegation payoff
- **Does:** sells effort-to-zero — the core fantasy of the whole video.
- **Word-level:** "**open up its own browser**" = the autonomy visual (the machine acts alone). "**the entire list**" = totalizer. "**while I just sit there**" = the zero-effort punchline (reused verbatim across videos), the payoff the whole ladder was climbing toward.

> "Here's the best part."
- **Component:** retention-spike (bonus flag)
- **Does:** signals over-delivery at the ~80% mark; keeps the viewer for one more beat.
- **Word-level:** "**the best part**" = superlative promise; 3 words, hard stop.

> "Say now recheck every site every week and resubmit any approval that gets ignored until completely gone."
- **Component:** mechanism-bonus (recurring-loop prompt)
- **Does:** promises the payoff never ends — automation that runs forever.
- **Word-level:** "**recheck every site every week**" = doubled totalizer + a cadence ("every... every"). "**resubmit any... that gets ignored**" = robustness. "**until completely gone**" = the finality end-state.

> "ChatGPT is gonna build you a scheduled task that runs every week until your identity is wiped out for good."
- **Component:** payoff (scheduled-task)
- **Does:** the ceiling-raise — a set-and-forget system, not a one-time trick.
- **Word-level:** "**build you a scheduled task**" = the concrete forever-artifact. "**runs every week**" = autonomy cadence. "**your identity is wiped out for good**" — "your identity" personal stakes, "wiped out for good" = the total, permanent end-state.

> "If you want all four of these prompts and more, make sure you follow me and comment, erase, and I'll shoot them over to you."
- **Component:** CTA (gate + follow)
- **Does:** converts curiosity into a comment + follow; the keyword is the title.
- **Word-level:** "**all four of these prompts and more**" = volume gate ("and more" = the extra bonus). "**follow me and comment**" = dual ask, follow first. "**erase**" = the one-word keyword = the title. "**I'll shoot them over to you**" = the tail (⚠️ Alex trims this).

**Formula extracted (fill-in-the-blank):**
> "What happens when you ask [AI] to [violent-verb] [your OWN personal asset] off [the place it lives]? I tried this and it actually [violent-verb] [scale: hundreds of X]. So first, open [AI] and say [find/list every X exposing me + give me the fix-link for each]. The results were terrifying. It found [specific personal data] being [violated].
