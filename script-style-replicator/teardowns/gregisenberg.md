## THE REPEATABLE VIRAL FORMULA (identical across every hit below)

### The copyable BEAT SKELETON
1. **Reframe hook** (1 sentence): name the tool in the first ~8 words, then tell the viewer they're currently doing it wrong / leaving value on the table. Interrogative, conditional, or "everyone's doing X" frame.
2. **Setup pivot** (1 short sentence): a doorway phrase that promises the how. "Here's the setup." / "Here's what you need to do instead." / "Here is how."
3. **Step 1 — foundation** ("Start by… / First,…"): imperative + named artifact + a plain-English "what it is," usually an analogy.
4. **Steps 2–3 — build-up** ("Next… / Then… / After that…"): each = imperative + artifact + one micro-benefit.
5. **The "But" escalation** (the re-hook): right when a step feels done, "But [X] alone isn't enough" resets curiosity and promises a deeper layer.
6. **Final step — the biggest** ("Finally… / And lastly…"): the most powerful capability, saved for last.
7. **Transformation line** (the cold close): a clean before → after payoff that cashes the hook's promise. No CTA.

### The EXACT transitional phrases reused VERBATIM across the videos
These are gregisenberg's load-bearing connective tissue. Quoted verbatim, with the videos they recur in:

- **Setup pivot:** `"Here's a setup that…"` (4 .md files) · `"Here's what you need to do instead."` (Claude Code Explained) · `"Here is how."` (OpenClaw money) — the `"Here's [X]"` doorway appears in 3 of 8, implied in the rest.
- **The staircase connectors, in strict order:** `"Start by…"` (4 .md, OpenClaw money) · `"First,…"` / `"But first,…"` (Claude Code Explained, Obsidian) · `"Next is…"` / `"Next, simply…"` (4 .md, Obsidian) · `"Then,…"` · `"After that,…"` (Obsidian) · `"Finally,…"` (Claude Code Explained, 100 FB ads) · `"And lastly,…"` (4 .md) · `"In the end, simply…"` (OpenClaw money) · `"From here,…"` (100 FB ads).
- **The "But" re-hook (the single most-repeated device, 7+ times across the set):** `"But feeding context alone isn't enough."` (4 .md) · `"But first,…"` (Obsidian) · `"But you can even take it a notch higher…"` (Cowork) · `"But what about the elephant in the room?"` (Cowork) · `"But that's not all."` (Cowork) · `"But, what's even cooler is…"` (Hermes) · `"But, a better way…"` (OpenClaw money).
- **Bonus-beat escalators:** `"But you can even take it a notch higher"` · `"what's even cooler"` · `"The best part?"` — the explicit "there's more value than you thought" flag.
- **Fragment spotlight (pacing jolt):** a one/two-word sentence used as a beat drop. `"Safety."` (Cowork) · `"The best part?"` (Hermes).
- **Transformation close:** `"That's how you can turn a 4-hour task into a single instruction."` (4 .md) · `"And that's how Claude connects the dots for you."` (Obsidian). The template is fixed: **`"That's how you can [BEFORE → AFTER]."`** / **`"And that's how [TOOL] [does the dream] for you."`**

### The DICTION FINGERPRINT (specific words + word-classes, every time)

- **Second person "you / your" — saturation.** It is the grammatical spine. 4 .md files: "you," "your business," "your preferences," "learns about you," "how you can" — the word "you/your" appears ~11 times in 144 words. Obsidian: "your notes," "your goals," "how you actually think," "for you" — ~10 times in 138 words. The viewer is always the subject.
- **Present tense + habitual present.** "Claude references that every time," "it links all your notes," "AI starts forgetting," "it logs that," "which it continuously updates." Nothing is hypothetical; the tool acts *now*.
- **Imperative verbs, one per step (the artifact-delivery verbs).** Counted across the set: *Start by, create, use, download, connect, ask, build, break it, test, watch, generate, feed, push, deploy, map out, set, hook, stack, run, install, grant.* Roughly 4–6 imperatives per video.
- **Contractions everywhere (spoken-register glue).** "Here's," "It's," "isn't," "that's," "Everyone's," "don't," "you've," "you're," "there's." Zero formal expansions. This is what makes it sound like a person, not a manual.
- **Hard numbers as anchors (≥1 in 8/8, usually 2–3).** "10% of its brain," "4-hour task," "single instruction," "under 1 minute," "100 Facebook ads / under 30 minutes," "200,000 tokens," "40 to 50%," "$100 token burn into $10," "5 to 10 micro startups." Numbers are the credibility receipts.
- **Named concrete artifacts (specificity = proof).** agents.md, context folder, memory.md, skills folder, Obsidian, vault, Obsidian CLI, `today` command, `emerge` command, ask-user-question tool, context window, Perplexity API, Facebook Ads API, Railway. He never says "a file"; he says *the memory.md file*.
- **Analogy nouns (the 12-year-old parse gloss).** "onboarding an employee," "a magic wand," "a third date," "your best thinking partner," "an entire web of how you think," "notes become its brain." Every abstract artifact gets one legible image.
- **Intensifiers / minimizers that shrink effort.** "literally" ("you literally walk through a process once"), "simply" ("Next, simply connect this"), "just" ("Claude just became"), "actually" ("how you actually think"), "even" ("you can even take it a notch higher"). These make the payoff feel free and the friction feel gone.
- **Comma-stacked triads (concretize by listing 3).** "your business, voice, and how you like to work" · "your calendar, tasks, and recent notes" · "every single technical detail, trade-off, and edge case" · "the scraping, the formatting, and the uploading."
- **Deliberately LEFT OUT:** no em-dashes (0 across all transcripts), no first-person "I" except the podcast clip, no hedging ("maybe," "kind of," "I think" — again only the podcast clip), no filler intros ("in this video I'll show you"), no sign-off/like-subscribe CTA.

### The VIRALITY LEVERS pulled every single time (ranked by recurrence)
1. **Open loop via loss-frame** — the hook makes you feel behind ("using 10% of its brain," "feels like slop," "forgets your name"), which you must watch to close. 8/8.
2. **The numbered staircase as a progress bar** — "Start by… Next… Finally…" gives the brain a countable path, and the last step is flagged as the biggest, so you stay. 8/8.
3. **Specificity as proof** — named files, named APIs, named commands. The concreteness reads as "this person actually did it." 8/8.
4. **Manufactured secondary curiosity gaps** — the "But [real magic]" pivots plant a second and third hook inside a 150-word script. 7/8.
5. **The transformation payoff** — a clean before → after line rewards completion and is the screenshot/share moment. 8/8.
6. **Number/timeframe stakes** — a hard metric ("100 ads in 30 minutes," "4-hour → single instruction") makes the promise falsifiable and therefore believable.

- **Word-level:** "Why would you" is second-person and accusatory-but-gentle. "subscribe" names the sunk cost (they pay for this). "Claude" is the tool, named by word 5. "only to use 10% of its brain" — the number **10%** is the fingerprint: a concrete loss metric, and "brain" is the analogy that anthropomorphizes the tool so wasting it feels dumb. No verb is wasted; the whole status quo is attacked in 13 words.

> "Here's a setup that makes it worth the buy."
- **Component:** setup pivot
- **Does:** closes the guilt loop with a promise and reframes the coming steps as ROI recovery, not homework.
- **Word-level:** "Here's" (contraction, doorway word) signals the how is starting *now*. "a setup" is vague-on-purpose (curiosity intact). "worth the buy" pays back the "subscribe" from the hook, closing the money frame. Short: 8 words.

> "Start by creating the agents.md file."

> "Your business, voice, and how you like to work."
- **Component:** mechanism-1 detail (concretizing triad)
- **Does:** shows exactly what goes in the file without handing over a template — sold, not spelled out.
- **Word-level:** a fragment (no verb) used as a spotlight list. The **comma-stacked triad** "business, voice, and how you like to work" is the signature concretizer. "Your" x2 keeps it personal.

- **Does:** shows the file is always-on, so the small setup has permanent leverage.

- **Component:** mechanism-2 (build-up step)
- **Does:** advances the progress bar; short beat keeps pace fast.
- **Word-level:** "Next is" — the sequence connector. 5 words, deliberately clipped. "the context folder" — named artifact again.

> "This contains heavier and more nuanced information."
- **Component:** mechanism-2 gloss
- **Does:** differentiates it from step 1 (escalating depth) without a jargon dump.
- **Word-level:** "heavier and more nuanced" is a comparative that signals you're going *deeper* — escalation baked into the adjective.

> "But feeding context alone isn't enough."
