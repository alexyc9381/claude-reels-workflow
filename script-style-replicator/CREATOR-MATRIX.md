## 2. The 8 voiceprints (side by side)

- **raycfu** — A calm insider leaning across the table to hand you one secret: "Most people don't realize [number]… here's how," then 3-5 crisply named steps each with a why and a dollar figure, prompts gated behind one word.
- **mavgpt** — A hyped tour guide who opens on a scary-or-magic yes/no question, walks you click-by-click through a live screen in short imperatives ("open Claude and say… now here's the trick"), spiking every 8-10s with a 4-word shock beat, prompts gated.
- **nateherk** — A fast, confident builder-to-builder show-and-tell that opens on the finished result, narrates the screen in real time with "boom" reactions, stacks real numbers and named companies, gates the full method.
- **nicksaraev** — A mildly-annoyed ex-agency guy who news-pegs the drop, calls the hype BS, deflates the complexity ("it's literally just a prompt"), drops hard numbers as receipts, then dares you to go cash in.
- **cindiezhu** — A warm "let me put you on" friend who opens "Did you know Claude can now ___?", kills your objection before you raise it ("before you spend thousands, try this"), and re-translates every feature into a plain outcome with "which means."
- **gregisenberg** — A calm builder-explainer who reframes a tool with one provocation ("using 10% of its brain"), walks you down a numbered "Start by… Next… Finally…" staircase where every step is an imperative naming a concrete artifact and its micro-payoff, pivots on "But [the real magic]," and lands on a single before→after transformation line.
- **rileybrown** — A live-build hype narrator who blurts an awe word ("Oh my god", "No way"), claims an impossible pairing ("this can literally control Blender"), speaks a rough prompt out loud, reacts to every output ("Boom", "Let's go"), escalates with "but I want to change X," and ships to a real public link.
- **sabrinaramonov** — A calm, credentialed money operator who tells "you" the one sequence "most people" get wrong, drops exact dollar-and-hours receipts, teases the live tool with "now here's the trick" then "here's the best part," and gates the full prompt set behind one word.

**The spine as a fill-in template:**

```
[GAP HOOK: novelty/secret/result/news/question/reframe/awe/tool-shock that lands VALUE NOUN by word ~12, with a number]
[FRAME FLIP: promise the steps / undercut the hard way / preempt the objection / name the artifact]
[MECHANISM 1: name it → what it does in plain words → why that matters / the outcome]
[MECHANISM 2: name it → what → outcome]
[MECHANISM 3: name it → what → outcome]
[RETENTION SPIKE: "the most important part / here's the trick / the wild part / But the real magic is" → the differentiator]
[RECEIPT: a third-person role runs this and gets a specific number]
[GATED CTA: Comment [ONE-WORD KEYWORD].  ← hard stop, no tail]
```

**What's universal across all eight (the invariants):**
1. Value noun by ~word 12, a **number lives in the hook**, no throat-clearing (first word is the hook).
2. The body is **named chunks**, not prose — steps/personas/agents/prompts you can picture.
3. **High number density** — a figure every few sentences; specific beats vague.
4. **Third-person receipts**, never a first-person flex (this is what makes all eight Alex-compatible once de-I'd).
5. **Gate the how** — show the architecture + outcome, withhold the exact prompt/file/config.
6. **One-word keyword CTA**, cut hard. (greg has no native CTA and riley/sabrina/mavgpt keep a tail; all get grafted/trimmed to a hard keyword stop.)

## 4. FUSION PROTOCOL (topic X + creator Y → finished Alex reel)

A literally-followable algorithm. Run top to bottom.

**Step 0 — Inputs.** You have `[TOPIC X]` (from a topic-ideas doc or the Outlier Engine) and, if unspecified, run the CREATOR ROUTER (§5) to pick `[CREATOR Y]`.

**Step 1 — Load creator DNA.** Open `creators/<Y>-dna.md`. Pull §1 (opener template + variant slot), §2 (beat map), §3 (per-beat micro-templates), §5 (phrase bank), §11 (ready-to-run template).

**Step 2 — Fill the §11 template.** Fire that creator's §11 skeleton with TOPIC X in every slot. Use their §3 micro-templates for each beat and salt in 2-4 verbatim phrases from their §5 bank so it *sounds* like Y (raycfu's "the most important part"; mavgpt's "every single"; nate's "boom" + one anaphora reveal; nick's "here's the thing everyone overcomplicates" + rule of three; cindie's "which means"; greg's "Start by… Next… Finally…" + "But the real magic is"; riley's "Oh my god / No way / Boom / Let's go"; sabrina's "Now here's the trick" → "Here's the best part"). This produces a raw draft in Y's native voice — including Y's rule-breaking habits.

**Step 3 — Apply Alex's HARD RULES as explicit transforms.** Run the raw draft through these, in order. Each is a find-and-fix, not a vibe:

1. **Strip em-dashes.** Replace every `—` with a comma, period, or colon. (Rule 1. Most creators' ASR has none; cindie's/sabrina's cadence risks them.)
2. **De-first-person → third-person receipt.** Find every "I tried this / I built / I used to / how I set this up / I sold my company." Rewrite:
   - anecdote loop-closer (mavgpt's/sabrina's "I tried this and it…") → `People are running this and [same visceral result]` — keep the affect word ("actually/literally"), kill the "I."
   - credential flex (nick's "I used to sell websites for a living"; sabrina's "I sold my AI company at 30") → the general truth: `This used to cost $10K a pop.` / drop the credential or make it general ("this is the stuff operators quietly use").
   - capability flex (nate's "I built these"; riley's "I plugged this into my Slack"; greg's "I actually think") → `Someone just built four of these in 15 minutes` OR a role receipt: `a coach ran this and…` / second-person "you point one agent at your Slack."
   - (Rule 2 + Rule 6: the receipt must be true/general, never an invented specific run or a quote in a named person's mouth.)
3. **Gate the how.** Find any place the VO hands over a complete, replicable method (mavgpt/raycfu speak full prompts; nick gives the repo away free; greg spells out every file; riley/sabrina voice full prompts on screen). Replace the word-for-word method with the *shape* + the artifact name, and move the exact text behind the keyword: `The full prompt is the part that actually makes this work.` Exception: rough partial spoken prompts in a follow-along ARE allowed as value; the full set is still gated. (Rule 3.)
4. **Cut on the keyword.** Delete every tail after the CTA keyword — raycfu's "follow for more advice…", mavgpt's "and I'll shoot them over to you", nate's "click the play button / thanks guys", cindie's "and I'll send it over", nick's "catch y'all next time", riley's "Uploaded it on YouTube", sabrina's "and I'll DM you". For greg, who ends cold on a transformation line, *append* the keyword CTA after compressing the vision line into a receipt. Last word of the script = the keyword. (Rule 4.)
5. **De-jargon (12-year-old parse) + value noun by ~word 12.** Swap any term a 12-year-old wouldn't get: "MCP/connector/webhook" → "one connection you set up once"; "orchestrator" → "the boss agent"; "out-of-sample gate" → "a test on data it's never seen"; "ICIR/validation loss" → "a consistency score"; riley's "noode backend / Expo token" → plain outcome. Keep model names (Claude, Opus) as flavor, not requirements. Confirm the value noun (the money/hours/customers outcome) lands by word ~12. (Rule 5.)
6. **Length.** Trim to `words ≤ target_seconds × 4.3` (35-45s ≈ 150-190 words; listicles may run 55-70s). (Rule 5.)
7. **Breadth litmus.** Read it as a freelance designer, a Shopify owner, a coach, AND a founder. If only a technical founder can act, broaden the topic or the receipt. (Rule 7. greg's Qwen-routing and riley's Blender builds skew technical — pick artifacts all four personas can use: a support agent, an app from a sketch, a money-leak audit.)

**Step 4 — Self-check against the Script Factory gates.** Do not skip; these are the same bars Stage 1/3/4 enforce (§6 maps the handoff):
- **Stage-1 kill-gate (topic sanity):** value noun is money/time/or a screenshot-able deliverable; audience-size score ≥8 on the *harsh* qualifier reading; payoff is a visually-legible artifact changing state in <2s sound-off (cerebral-payoff test); opening lever isn't the same family as the last 2 in `memory/reel-lever-ledger.md`. Any FAIL → fix the topic before writing more.
- **Hook gate (`memory/claude-reel-hook-library.md`, all 10 checks):** drafted 10+ first lines, scored, picked top 2-3 across ≥2 hook families; punch in sentence ONE; mute-readable number/contrast on frame 1; specificity baits the STAKE not the tool; a verified family (not a KILLED pattern — no identity-callout, no "nobody's talking about this," no product-showcase); every curiosity/question loop is paid off later; sounds human. If top two hooks are within 1 point, present both to Alex.
- **Stage-3 draft constraints:** re-confirm ≤ target×4.3 words, zero em-dashes, but/so causality, gate-the-how held, any surviving first-person claim flagged TRUTH-REQUIRED (or reworded out now).

**Step 5 — Output.** Deliver the finished ~35-45s script + the keyword + a one-line note of which creator flavor and hook variant fired. This is a Stage-3 draft ready to enter the Stage-4 adversarial gate.

**One-paragraph version:** pick creator via router → open `creators/<Y>-dna.md` → fire its §11 template on the topic with 2-4 phrases from §5 → run the 7 HARD-RULE transforms (strip em-dashes, de-I to third-person receipt, gate the how, cut on keyword, de-jargon + value-noun-by-12, length, breadth) → self-check against Stage-1 kill-gate + the 10-point hook gate + Stage-3 constraints → ship the draft to Stage 4.

## 5. CREATOR ROUTER (topic shape → whose DNA to borrow)

Read the topic's *shape*, then borrow that creator's DNA. The spine (§3) is constant; you're choosing the flavor that fits the topic's natural energy.

**Quick decision list (first match wins):**
1. Topic is **"point Claude at YOUR data and watch it expose + fix something," doable tonight, prompt-shaped** → **mavgpt** (Q-Hook follow-along; info→artifact→agent→loop ladder). *e.g. "find every subscription draining your account."*
2. Topic is a **named multi-agent money system / a build with 3-5 nameable roles / "here's the machine"** → **raycfu** (insider-gap + named-step grammar). *e.g. "the 3-agent setup that runs your inbox."*
3. Topic is a **frontier capability, a new skill/tool, or a "just dropped" news moment with a finished artifact to show** → **nateherk** (result-first or news-drop + enabler name-drop + screen walkthrough). *e.g. "the connector that plugs Claude into everything you own."*
4. Topic is a **$-result / receipts build, an overnight optimizer loop, or "this kills your $/mo tool"** → **nicksaraev** (news-peg + skeptic-deflate + hard-number receipts + scale-out). *e.g. "the cold email that improves itself while you sleep."*
5. Topic is a **persona/outcome for the broadest, least-technical audience — "turn Claude into a named specialist/team," mute-readable, zero-jargon** → **cindiezhu** (novelty question + objection-preempt + persona listicle + "which means"). *e.g. "the 5 agents that run a founder's marketing team."*
6. Topic is a **numbered-promise setup / an ordered "here's the N-file / N-step build," a leverage stack with a clean staircase and a before→after payoff** → **gregisenberg** (reframe hook + "Start by… Next… Finally…" staircase + "But the real magic" + transformation line). *e.g. "the 4 .md files that make Claude actually worth the subscription," "the setup that turns a 4-hour task into one instruction."*
7. Topic is a **live vibe-coding awe-demo — build-it-on-screen, an impossible pairing, ship-it-to-a-real-link, "this shouldn't be possible"** → **rileybrown** (awe interjection + capability claim + prompt→react→escalate loop + ship). *e.g. "Claude turned a napkin sketch into a live app in three prompts."*
8. Topic is a **make-money-with-AI sequence / playbook — an hours-saved or dollars-made outcome, a money-leak audit, or a ranked "N ways to make money" list** → **sabrinaramonov** (tool-shock hook + receipt + "Now here's the trick / Here's the best part" + gate). *e.g. "the overnight agent team that audits your business for money leaks."*

**Tie-breakers / overlaps:**
- **Multi-agent teams** qualify for raycfu (name the machine), cindie (persona listicle), mavgpt (spin up the team as the "trick" beat), OR sabrina (money-outcome framing). Pick by audience: raycfu = builder who wants the architecture; cindie = broad/non-technical; mavgpt = doable-tonight follow-along; sabrina = the money/hours receipt is the point.
- **Overnight / autonomous** fits mavgpt (the escalation ladder tops out here), nick (the autoresearch loop), nate (Fable "one prompt, walked away"), cindie (Sub-lane C), greg (the overnight-loop step on the staircase), AND sabrina (the "run while you sleep" money audit). Pick by whether the payoff is a **loop with a metric** (nick), a **live screen artifact** (nate), a **doable follow-along** (mavgpt), a **named team** (cindie), an **ordered setup** (greg), or a **money/hours receipt** (sabrina).
- **News-peg / "just dropped"** → nate (react + show the artifact), nick (react + deflate the hype), or sabrina (tool-shock "just dropped something insane, nobody knows"). Choose nick when the honest move is skepticism; nate when the move is awe at a real result; sabrina when the move is "here's the money it saves you." **All carry a hard freshness gate** — only ship while the drop is genuinely current.
- **Contrarian / "your AI is lying to you" / "the hyped thing is theater"** → nick (Lane D, evidenced skeptic-deflate), cindie (Sub-lane D, "stop the yes-man" behavior fix), OR greg ("everyone's using Claude Code like a magic wand, here's what to do instead"). nick for a public wave; cindie for a personal behavior config; greg when the fix is an ordered setup.
- **Build-it-live / "watch it happen"** → riley (awe vibe-coding stream, ship to a link) or nate (builder show-and-tell, artifact on screen). riley when the energy is real-time awe and escalation; nate when it's a calmer result-first walkthrough with receipts.
- **Ordered how-to / "here's the setup in N steps"** → greg (numbered staircase, imperative + artifact per step) or mavgpt (temporal follow-along with spoken prompts). greg when steps are files/artifacts you configure once; mavgpt when steps are prompts you type live tonight.
- **Make-money listicle / "N ways to make money"** → sabrina ("Path number N" money grammar) or raycfu (category/numbered grammar with a why). sabrina for the money-first framing; raycfu when each path needs an architecture.
- **Skills / slash-command roundups** → cindie (persona listicle), raycfu (category/numbered grammar), or greg (numbered-file staircase). cindie for breadth; raycfu when each skill needs a "why it matters"; greg when the roundup is an ordered setup.

**⭐ Measured tie-breaker (once data exists):** the topic-shape rules above are the *prior*. As soon as
`perf/TRANSFER-SCORES.md` has n≥3 for a creator (built from `PERFORMANCE-LOG.md`), it becomes the
primary tie-breaker: when two creators fit a topic, pick the one with the higher measured avg-watch on
Alex's own account. Raw creator views prove the style worked for THEM; the transfer score proves it
works for Alex's faceless mascot. Re-run `perf/compute_transfer_scores.py` after each post autopsy.
