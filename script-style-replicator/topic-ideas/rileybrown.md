## Sub-lane A — Agent controls an unexpected app (the impossible pairing)

### A1. "Your AI Agent Can Now Run Your Whole Spreadsheet"
- **Hook (spoken first line):** "No way. One agent can now open your spreadsheet, clean it, and chart it while you watch."
- **Extends:** OpenClaw→Blender impossible-pairing, swapped to a tool everyone touches (a spreadsheet, not Blender).
- **Alex-niche fit:** autonomous agent given control of a real app the viewer already uses daily; outcome = hours back on reporting.
- **Breadth check:** PASS. Freelancer, Shopify owner, coach, founder all live in spreadsheets.
- **Frontier/fresh:** agent-controls-a-desktop-app is bleeding edge; nobody frames it around the boring spreadsheet everyone dreads. White space vs Riley's niche Blender build.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** the "wait, an agent can drive a real app I use?" gap fires just as hard on Excel as on Blender, but reaches 50x more people.
- **§11 template:** full skeleton; weight on ESCALATION ("now make it a live dashboard").
- **KILL-LIST risk:** low. Keep "spreadsheet" as value noun by word 8; do NOT drift to "control Blender" jargon.

### A2. "It Booked My Calendar, My Email, and My Reminders in One Go"
- **Hook:** "This shouldn't be possible. You point one agent at your calendar and inbox and it just runs your whole morning."
- **Extends:** the agent-drives-real-apps pairing; multiplied to a chain of daily apps at once.
- **Alex-niche fit:** autonomous agent + big-context over your real accounts; outcome = the admin hour gone.
- **Breadth check:** PASS. Everyone has a calendar and an inbox.
- **Frontier/fresh:** multi-app orchestration in one shot, not a single-tool demo.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** stacking three familiar apps into one command reproduces Riley's "many things at once" MCP awe without the "noode backend" jargon.
- **§11 template:** weight on the ROUGH SPOKEN PROMPT (the plain "just tell it" tease) since the wow IS the plain-language command.
- **KILL-LIST risk:** "This shouldn't be possible" is Riley's literal title — flag as near-cliché; rotate to "No way" or "Watch this" if the hook library shows it's saturated. Also strip any first-person "my calendar" → "your calendar."

### A3. "An Agent Just Redesigned This Website by Itself"
- **Hook:** "Look at this. An agent just took over a real website and redesigned the whole page live."
- **Extends:** agent-controls-a-visual-app (Blender→web design), the design-surface version.
- **Alex-niche fit:** autonomous agent on a visual build; outcome = a designer's afternoon in minutes.
- **Breadth check:** PASS (leans creative but a coach or store owner wants a better page too). Keep the ask generic ("your landing page") not "your React repo."
- **Frontier/fresh:** live autonomous redesign, not a static "I made a site" reel.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** the visual-change-happening-live loop (Riley's "it's too big, now too small, okay it figured it out") is the most re-watchable beat; a website redesign keeps that live-correction drama and is broader than Blender.
- **§11 template:** weight on WAIT+REACT (the live self-correction is the retention engine).
- **KILL-LIST risk:** low. Watch the breadth edge — do not tip into "component library / repo" jargon.

## Batch-level KILL-LIST / freshness flags
- **"This shouldn't be possible"** appears on A2, C8, E14. It is Riley's literal 1M title and risks reading as a borrowed cliché. Ship at most ONE per batch; rotate the others to "No way" or "Watch this."
- **First-person leakage** is the top adaptation risk in every C and B idea (Riley's native voice is "my Slack / I'm working on this video"). Every hook above is pre-cast to "you / your"; enforce on final scripts.
- **Jargon offenders to strip** before Stage-1: MCP, noode backend, GitHub, Vercel, Expo, localhost, repo, React (present in C7, C8, D11, E13). Say "give it access to your apps," "paste the tool," "it's live on your phone."
- **Breadth watch-list:** B6 (video editor), D11 (GitHub link), D12 (game) skew narrow. Keep in the pipeline but behind the PASS ideas; give each a stated operator outcome so it clears the litmus.
