## Lane A - The scary Q-Hook business audit (extends ERASE / PHOTO / IDENTITY)
The flagship. Same skeleton as the outliers: a scary yes/no question about YOUR business -> agent finds the hidden thing -> writes the fix -> a browser/agent does it itself -> a scheduled task loops it forever. Money/customers outcome. All fire **DNA template: §11 Q-Hook 3-prompt follow-along** (info -> artifact -> autonomous agent -> scheduled loop).

### A1. Every Customer About to Cancel on You
- **Hook (mavgpt voice):** "What happens when you ask Claude to find every customer who's about to cancel on you before they even do it?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** Claude + payments/support MCP flags churn-risk accounts, drafts win-back, agent sends, scheduled weekly re-scan. Pure agents + MCP + loop.
- **Breadth:** designer on retainer, Shopify seller, coach with a cohort, SaaS founder all have churn.
- **Frontier/fresh:** connectors reading live Stripe/support data + an autonomous send loop is new; not a dated "read my spreadsheet."
- **Transfer hypothesis:** money-at-risk framing is a stronger gasp than mavgpt's privacy gasp because it maps to lost revenue, and the whole payoff is a screen recording of a churn list building live, which is exactly what a mascot reel shows best.

### A2. Every Unpaid Invoice Hiding in Your Inbox
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to dig up every unpaid invoice people still owe you and chase all of them for you?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** agent scans inbox/accounting, builds the owed-money list, drafts each chase, browser sends, then follows up on a loop until paid.
- **Breadth:** freelancer, agency, coach, founder all get ghosted on invoices.
- **Frontier/fresh:** the autonomous follow-up loop (not a one-shot draft) is the frontier hook.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "money people already owe you" is a loss-frame with zero setup cost to understand, and the brutal total ("this many dollars outstanding") is a totalizer number-drop that lands cold on a caption card.

### A3. The Competitor You're Not Watching Closely Enough
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to watch every competitor you have and ping you the second any of them change anything?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** overnight agent monitors pricing/launches/hiring, big-context diff, scheduled daily digest.
- **Breadth:** any designer, seller, coach, founder has competitors.
- **Frontier/fresh:** always-on overnight monitoring agent is squarely the autonomous/scheduled frontier.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "while you sleep, it watches your rivals" is the delegation fantasy mavgpt sells ("while you just sit there") applied to paranoia, and a mascot asleep while a feed scrolls is a clean visual.

### A4. Every Lead You Let Go Cold
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to find every lead you ghosted and quietly warm all of them back up?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** point at CRM/inbox, agent surfaces dead threads, drafts a tailored re-open per lead, sends on a loop.
- **Breadth:** everyone who sells anything has a cold-lead graveyard.
- **Frontier/fresh:** per-lead custom re-engagement at scale via agent, not a mail-merge, is the fresh angle.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "customers you already paid to get and then lost" is a sharp loss-frame, and the escalation to an agent that sends them itself is the dopamine beat that reads on screen.

### A5. Every Bad Review Quietly Costing You Customers
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to hunt down every bad review of you across the internet and handle each one?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** near-identical mechanics to PHOTO's reverse-search: agent finds reviews everywhere, drafts a response to each, browser posts, weekly re-scan.
- **Breadth:** local service, Shopify store, coach, agency all live and die on reviews.
- **Frontier/fresh:** the browser-agent that actually replies (not just finds) is the escalation frontier.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** this is the closest structural clone of a proven 735K outlier (find-every-X-about-you), just retargeted from photos to reviews, so the transfer risk is lowest of the batch.

## Lane B - The build-it-once slash Skill (extends HUMAN 246K)
mavgpt's HUMAN win ends the escalation not in an agent but in a **/slash Skill built via skill creator** that fires forever. This is uniquely Alex's frontier (Skills + slash commands) and gates cleanly. **DNA template: §11 Q-Hook follow-along, but the "Now here's the trick" beat = the slash skill-creator build** (mirror HUMAN's beat 7 exactly).

### B1. The Skill That Writes in Your Exact Voice Forever
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to build you a skill so it writes in your exact voice every single time?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** Claude studies your best posts, builds a `/voice` Skill, then every draft ships on-brand with one slash. Skills frontier.
- **Breadth:** every creator, freelancer, coach, founder writes content in a voice.
- **Frontier/fresh:** a reusable installed Skill (not a copy-paste prompt) is the new mechanic most people haven't seen.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "build it once, never prompt again" is a stronger promise than a single output, and typing `/voice` on a real screen is a satisfying one-second payoff shot for a mascot reel.

### B2. The One Command That Builds a Full Sales Page
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to build one command that spits out an entire sales page every time you type it?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** a `/salespage` Skill encoding your offer, proof, and structure; one slash = full page.
- **Breadth:** anyone launching a product, service, or course needs a sales page.
- **Frontier/fresh:** encoding a repeatable deliverable as a Skill is the fresh capability.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** a whole page materializing from one command is a bigger visible gap than "AI wrote some copy," and the slash-to-artifact beat carries with no face needed.

### B3. The Command That Onboards Every New Client for You
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to build one command that runs your entire client onboarding for you?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** `/onboard` fires the welcome, the intake doc, the kickoff plan, the folder setup in one shot; Skills + light agent.
- **Breadth:** agency, freelancer, coach, consultant all onboard clients.
- **Frontier/fresh:** a Skill that chains a multi-step workflow (not one asset) is the frontier.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "hours of admin collapse into one slash" is an hours-saved outcome that survives transfer because the win is the command firing, not a talking head explaining it.

## Lane C - The death-claim capability tour (extends DESIGN 219K)
Present-tense "Claude just killed [profession] so you can now [do the thing they charged for]" + meta-promise + examples stack + price anchor + CTA. **DNA template: §2 capability-tour variant** (one prompt or none; the live demo carries it; end on the "charging thousands, Claude does it free" anchor).

### C1. Claude Just Killed the $5,000 Brand Package
- **Hook:** "Claude just killed brand designers, so you can now build an entire brand identity yourself in an afternoon."
- **Alex-niche fit:** Claude Design/Code generates logo directions, palette, type, and a mini brand guide live; price anchor baked in.
- **Breadth:** any founder, seller, coach, or freelancer needs a brand.
- **Frontier/fresh:** ties to Claude's newer design surface, not a generic "AI logo" tool.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** the price anchor ("designers charge thousands, this is free") is mavgpt's proven re-hook and it lands harder as a caption stat than in-mouth, so it transfers up on a mascot reel.
- **KILL-LIST WATCH:** death-claim tours drift toward **generic product/outcome showcase**. Must anchor on ONE specific gasp artifact (the full brand guide appearing in 3 minutes) + the price anchor, not a vague "look what Claude can do" montage.

### C2. Claude Just Killed the Market-Research Firm
- **Hook:** "Claude just killed market research firms, so you can now get a full report on any market in ten minutes."
- **Alex-niche fit:** big-context research agent pulls a sized-up market, competitors, pricing, gaps into one deliverable.
- **Breadth:** anyone deciding what to build/sell/charge.
- **Frontier/fresh:** big-context + agentic research is the fresh capability vs a static "ask AI a question."
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "a $10k report for free in ten minutes" is a clean hours+money outcome, and a report assembling on screen is inherently faceless-friendly.
- **KILL-LIST WATCH:** same generic-showcase risk; anchor on the finished report artifact + a real time/price number.

### C3. Claude Just Killed the Virtual Assistant You Pay Monthly
- **Hook:** "Claude just killed the virtual assistant, so an agent now runs your inbox, calendar, and follow-ups while you sleep."
- **Alex-niche fit:** overnight agent + connectors + scheduled task; the VA replacement is the frontier flex.
- **Breadth:** every busy operator across the four personas.
- **Frontier/fresh:** autonomous overnight agent doing real inbox/calendar work is squarely frontier.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** replacing a recurring monthly cost is a concrete money-saved frame, and "while you sleep" is the exact delegation beat that reads on a mascot.
- **KILL-LIST WATCH:** keep it to a specific handled task with a visible result, not a broad "does everything" showcase.

## Lane D - The AI council / agent-team (extends the escalation ladder + STACK philosophy)
mavgpt's ladder tops out at ONE autonomous agent; Alex's frontier extends it to **many agents at once** (councils, agent teams). Fire **DNA template: §11 Q-Hook follow-along, with the "trick" beat = spinning up multiple agents** instead of one browser.

### D1. The Room of AI Experts That Tears Apart Your Business
- **Hook:** "What happens when you ask Claude to put five expert agents in a room and tear apart your business before you spend a dollar?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** an AI council (a skeptic, a CFO, a marketer, a customer) debates your plan and returns the holes. Pure council frontier.
- **Breadth:** anyone with a plan, offer, or pitch to pressure-test.
- **Frontier/fresh:** multi-agent debate is a capability most viewers have never seen run.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** "five experts in a room" is a vivid image a mascot reel can literally stage with five clay heads, and the harsh verdict is a shock-beat payoff.

### D2. The Agent Team That Runs Your Whole Launch Overnight
- **Hook:** "What happens when you build a team of Claude agents that run your entire product launch while you sleep?"
- **Alex-niche fit:** parallel agents handle copy, assets, scheduling, outreach; the trick beat = spawning the team; scheduled task loops it.
- **Breadth:** every persona launches something.
- **Frontier/fresh:** coordinated agent teams working in parallel overnight is the top of Alex's frontier.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** the "team working while you sleep" fantasy is mavgpt's delegation payoff at maximum scale, and a grid of agents lighting up is a strong faceless visual.

## Lane E - The outcome-menu tier list (extends STACK 349K)
No hook sentence; the escalating triad rhythm IS the hook: "For [use case], this is bad, this is good, this one [makes you rich]." **DNA template: §2 tier-list variant.** Keyword-gated full stack at the end.

### E1. The AI Money Stack for 2026
- **Hook (opens mid-list, no preamble):** "For finding clients, this one's useless, this one's fine, this one makes you rich."
- **Alex-niche fit:** each triad is a builder money-use-case (finding clients, closing, delivering, automating, scaling) with Claude/agents as the top tier; gate "my full 2026 money stack."
- **Breadth:** every persona wants the money use-cases ranked.
- **Frontier/fresh:** the top-tier picks are agents/MCP/Claude Code, keeping it frontier not evergreen-tool-roundup.
- **Transfer hypothesis:** the pattern-interrupt triad rhythm is format-driven, not face-driven, so it transfers to a mascot almost losslessly, and the money framing is Alex's white space.
- **KILL-LIST WATCH:** each rung must name a specific outcome, not vague "this is good" filler, or it slides toward a **generic product showcase**. Keep the tiers concrete and the money verbs sharp.

## Kill-list scan summary
- **No identity-callout hooks** ("If you're a founder, watch this") anywhere in the batch. All use the Q-Hook, death-claim, or tier-list openers, which are second-person-outcome, not identity-gated.
- **No "I went deep and found something nobody's talking about"** framing.
- **Generic product/outcome-showcase risk** flagged on **C1, C2, C3, and E1** (the capability tours and the tier list). Each must anchor on ONE specific gasp artifact + a real number/price, not a vague montage of everything Claude can do. Lane A and Lane B are lowest-risk because the follow-along mechanics force a concrete escalation.
