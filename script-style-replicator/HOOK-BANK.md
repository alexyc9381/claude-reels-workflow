## 0. How to use

This is a swipe file, not a copy file. Every line below already beat the scroll on a real account, so it is a proven *shape* — pull the shape, refit the [SLOTS] to your Claude/AI-for-founders topic, then run the rewrite through the 10-point HOOK GATE (`claude-reel-hook-library.md`) before you record. Higher view count = stronger signal that the shape travels, but **the raw numbers are NOT normalized across creators**: each creator has a different baseline audience. cindiezhu's corpus median is ~6.7K (the library pegs her typical median even lower, ~2–3K), so her 292K "Did you know…" outlier is a ~40–100x jump over her own baseline — a far bigger relative signal than mavgpt's 735K, which is only ~2x his 349K median. Read the *lift over that creator's median*, not just the headline number. When in doubt, trust a shape that outperformed for its own creator AND appears across multiple creators.

**Which shapes carry the most weight:** **Direct Promise has the highest median (154.5K)** — it's the safest, most repeatable hook. But the two absolute biggest hits are **Open-Loop Questions** (mavgpt 735K/442K), which is why that family has the highest peak and the lowest median at once: it's a boom-or-bust shape. **Do not read the medians naively.** Family medians are contaminated by creator baseline. And cindiezhu's 292K "Did you know…" is a **~44x lift over her own median** — the single biggest relative outlier in the corpus, a much louder signal than mavgpt's 735K (~2x his median). Always weight *lift over that creator's baseline*.

**Transferable lessons (steal these):**

- **A number is the mid-tier engine, a stake is the top-tier engine.** Only ~4 of the top 15 first-sentences carry a digit; the biggest hits run on a visceral concrete stake ("erase your identity," "every photo of you") or a news-drop. Roughly half of all 41 openers carry a hard number *somewhere* in the opener, and those cluster in the proof/promise families. Rule: use a number OR a visceral stake — never a vague tool-tease with neither.
- **The value noun lands by ~word 8–12, every time.** "erase your entire digital footprint" (w6–10), "a full color analysis" (w4–6), "4 AI agents… as the dev team" (w8–14), "an entire creative studio… with seven skills" (w7–12). If your concrete payoff isn't named by word 12, the hook is warming up — cut the warm-up.
- **Question-openers dominate the very top of the leaderboard** (mavgpt's "What happens when you ask Claude to…" is 3 of the top 9). But they're only as strong as the concreteness of the thing you ask, and they MUST pay the loop.
- **The news-drop is the most bankable shape.** "Anthropic just [launched/released/dropped] X" carried nick to a 194K median. When there's a real Claude/Anthropic product moment, lead with it flat — no tease needed.
- **The loss-frame is a free multiplier.** cindiezhu bolts "before you spend $3,000 / waste hours / pay an agency" onto her question, turning a capability into a stakes-of-inaction. Cheap to add, pushes saves/sends.
- **Hyper-Specific Number and Proof-First are white space in this niche.** Only 3 and 1 examples respectively — and Alex's HARD RULES (number-forward, real on-screen proof) are tailor-made for both. Under-supplied shape + rule-fit = where Alex can win uncrowded.

## 5. ⛔ Do NOT use (kill-list cross-check)

Patterns that tested **dead** in verification (`claude-reel-hook-library.md`) — never build a hook on these:

- **Identity-callout: "If you're a [founder/freelancer], watch this."** (scored 0–3.)
- **First-person investigator: "I went deep and found something nobody's talking about."** (0–3.)
- **Pure product/outcome showcase as the whole hook** — the claim that "showcase = highest performer" was falsified (0–3). Showcases still appear, but a bare "look what I made" without a number/promise attached does not carry.
- **Fabricated retention stats: "X% watch past 3 seconds."** (most are invented.)
- **Guardrail (Alex's audience): scammy income/follower flexes** — "$43→$2,109," "0 to 50k followers." Reads get-rich-quick to a founder audience; use domain-relevant proof shown on screen instead.

**Cross-check of the 41 corpus hooks:**

- ✅ **Identity-callout:** zero in the corpus. None open with "If you're a ___, watch this."
- ✅ **Fabricated "% watch past 3s":** zero in the corpus.
- ✅ **First-person investigator tease:** none use the vague "I went digging and found something nobody's talking about." BUT ⚠️ **flag — raycfu's "Nobody's talking about this, but…" (61K #5, 30K #6)** is the same neighborhood as the killed phrase. It *survives* only because he pays it off in the very next breath with a concrete receipt ("the agency model is obsolete — you keep 90%," "a team running 20 agents making $10M"). For Alex: the phrase is usable **only** with an instant on-screen proof; the killed version is the one that stays a vague tease. Never leave "nobody's talking about this" unpaid.
- ⚠️ **Product/outcome showcase:** nateherk's "What you're looking at right here is 36 videos…" (64K) and "Here's how I build…" openers are showcase-adjacent — but each is rescued by a **precise number** or an explicit **"here's how" promise**, never a bare showcase. Copy the number/promise, not the showcase.
- ⚠️ **Income/follower flex — the one real conflict.** Several raycfu hooks are income-flex-adjacent and should **NOT be copied verbatim** for Alex's founder audience: "$500 a day" (29K), "10 million dollars in revenue" (30K), "$80k/month" (title), "$50,000 per project… keep 90%" (61K). nateherk's "a brand new group of millionaires is being created by AI" (116K) is borderline but safer because the proof is third-person (a real company, Vulcan) rather than a personal cash claim. **Resolution:** keep the *shape* (a specific number as proof) but swap the money-you'll-make claim for a domain-relevant result shown on screen — real Claude output, ship logs, a before/after. Per SHARED-CONTEXT rule 2, a creator's "this makes me $X" becomes a third-person receipt ("this makes people $X"), never an invented first-person income story.
- ⚠️ **Freshness (Gate #10):** the corpus itself proves the rule — mavgpt's ERASE re-post (#11, **0 views**) is a near-duplicate opening line of his 442K IDENTITY hook. Same line, second time, did not travel. Never reuse a prior opening LINE or opening VISUAL.

Everything else in the bank is clear to pull, refit, and run through the 10-point HOOK GATE.
