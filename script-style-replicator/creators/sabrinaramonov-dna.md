## 1. Opener / Hook Formula

### Primary template (TikTok — the format Alex uses)
Two flavors carry the short-form outliers:

**A. The "just dropped / nobody knows" tool-shock** (highest-signal for Alex's niche)
> `[TOOL] just [dropped/can now do] [something insane] that will literally [OUTCOME: save you N hours / make you money] and nobody knows about it.`

Verbatim, *Claude routines — 11.4k*:
> "Claude just dropped something insane that will literally save you 20 hours per week and nobody knows about it."

**B. The "What happens when you…" question + instant receipt**
> `What happens when you [do surprising thing with AI]? [Third-person/receipt: it actually did X].`

Verbatim, *I asked ChatGPT — 66.6k* (the top outlier) and *ChatGPT erases your digital identity — 18k*:
> "What happens when you ask ChatGPT to erase your entire digital footprint off the internet? I tried this and it actually wiped my identity off hundreds of websites."

### Count across all 9
- Tool-shock "just dropped…insane…nobody knows": 1 of 9 (Claude routines) — but the DNA fragments (insane / literally / N hours / nobody knows) recur everywhere.
- "What happens when you…?" question hook: 2 of 9 (both erase-footprint TikToks, incl. the 66.6k top video).
- "[X] are everywhere right now, but most people don't know…": 1 of 9 (Vibe coding tiers — 16.2k): "AI app builders are everywhere right now, but most people don't know which ones are actually good."
- "If you're [X] struggling / If you've tried [Y] and nothing stuck…" pain-conditional: 2 of 9 (both YouTube): "If you've tried to build income online and nothing stuck, you're starting in the wrong phase." / "If you're a business owner struggling to use AI to save time, make more money, and build leverage, you are not alone."
- "Today I'm going to show you [N] ways…": 1 of 9 (12 Ways — 22k): "Today I'm going to show you 12 ways to make money in 2026 using Claude. Ranked from the fastest first dollar to the most scalable income."
- Status-ladder reassurance: 1 of 9 (You're Not Behind — 14k): "You're not behind on AI, not even close… By the end of this video, you'll understand it better than 90% of them. And if you stay till the end, you'll be in the top 1%."

### The 3–5 rules that make the hook fire
1. **Name the tool + the shock in one breath.** Subject is Claude/ChatGPT, verb is "just dropped / can now / erase," and the shock word is present ("insane," "terrifying," "nobody knows").
2. **The value noun lands by ~word 10.** "erase your entire digital footprint," "save you 20 hours per week," "run a whole team of agents." Never a vague promise.
3. **Immediately follow the hook with a receipt.** The claim is proven in the very next sentence: "it actually wiped my identity off hundreds of websites." (For Alex this receipt becomes third-person — see §10.)
4. **Contrarian framing baked in.** "nobody knows about it," "most people don't know which ones are actually good" — the viewer is being let in on a secret.
5. **Outcome is time or money, always.** Every hook resolves to hours saved, dollars made, or leverage gained.

## 4. Sentence Rhythm & Mechanics

Measured on the TikTok scripts (the length that matches Alex):
- **Avg sentence length:** ~14–16 words, with hard swings. Instruction sentences run long (20–40 words: "You want to hit the plus, turn on agent mode and then say, visit each site and submit the removal request" = 21 words). Transition sentences are tiny: **"Now here's the trick." (4 words). "Here's the best part." (4 words).** This 4-word-beat / 20-word-instruction alternation is the signature cadence.
- **Opener length:** ~14 words ("What happens when you ask ChatGPT to erase your entire digital footprint off the internet?" = 14).
- **Imperative vs declarative:** TikTok is imperative-dominant, second person. The engine phrase is **"you want to…"** used as a soft command (3× in each erase-footprint script: "you want to open up chat," "You want to hit the plus," "You want all four of these prompts"). Also bare imperatives: "open up chat and say," "stay in the same chat," "hit follow," "comment erase." YouTube tilts declarative-explanatory but keeps second-person direct address ("you're going to binge-watch everything," "all you have to do is get 200 people").
- **Number / $ density:** Extreme. Money and hours are the texture. YouTube: "$50,000 per month," "$3,000 next week," "$1,200," "$15,000," "$50 per month… 200 people… 10K per month," "5 to 20x revenue multiple," "1 to 20 million," "$29 per month," "save 10 hours per week," "80X growth," "32 million views." TikTok is lighter but keeps one anchor number ("20 hours per week," "all four of these prompts," "six AI tools"). **Rule: at least one dollar figure or hour-savings per 30 seconds.**
- **Causal connectors:** Heavy "so / but / and / because." Sentences and beats *open* with them: "So first," "So that's," "But first," "And here's the best part," "The reason why is," "This is why nothing is working because…"
- **Signature punctuation/cadence:** Colon-led prompts ("say: search the internet for everything about me"). Rhetorical question then answer ("How do you find coaching clients? You can find coaching clients like everywhere on social media."). Triple repetition for emphasis ("the hardest, hardest, hardest part," "get 200 people. 200 people paying $50 per month"). No em-dashes needed — she already leans on commas and periods.

## 6. Retention / Escalation Devices
- **The two-stage reveal: "Now here's the trick." → "Here's the best part."** This is the core re-hook. Trick = the clever unlock (agent mode, a schedule); best part = the hands-off/compounding payoff. It re-baits attention twice in a 40s clip.
- **"nobody knows / most people get it wrong" secret framing** — positions the viewer as an insider getting the edge.
- **Status ladder** — "better than 90% of them… top 1% if you stay till the end" (You're Not Behind) explicitly rewards watch-time.
- **Running number-drops** — every 20–30s a dollar or hour figure re-anchors the stakes ("$50,000 per month," "save 20 hours per week").
- **Escalation cliffhanger between beats (YouTube)** — "the next rule makes or breaks everything else," "there's still one mistake most people make that cancels all the progress." Each promises the next section is the important one.
- **"do the math" mini-proofs** — "$50 per month, 200 people, that's 10K per month recurring." Concrete arithmetic makes the outcome feel inevitable.

## 9. Why It Works (transferable mechanism)
- **She sells the outcome, not the tool.** Every hook and offer resolves to hours saved or dollars made ("save 20 hours per week"), so a non-technical viewer never has to understand the tech to want it.
- **The two-stage "trick / best part" reveal manufactures a mid-clip attention spike** exactly where short-form viewers drop off.
- **"Most people get this wrong" makes the viewer feel late but rescuable** — urgency without shame, which is highly shareable/save-able.
- **Partial prompts given freely + full set gated** delivers real value on-screen (earning trust) while creating a reason to comment (earning the algorithm signal and the DM funnel).
- **Concrete arithmetic ("$50 × 200 = $10K/mo")** makes an aspirational outcome feel like a solved equation, not a dream.

## 10. Adapting to Alex (the fusion layer)

### What transfers cleanly
- The **tool-shock hook** ("Claude just dropped something insane that will save you N hours and nobody knows about it") is perfect for a faceless Claude mascot + screen-record — it names Claude and an outcome in one line.
- The **"So first → then → Now here's the trick → Here's the best part → keyword"** skeleton maps 1:1 onto a 40s screen-record walkthrough.
- **Outcome-in-hours/dollars framing** matches Alex's outcome-framed niche exactly.
- **The keyword-comment CTA** is already Alex's mechanic.
- **Partial-prompt-on-screen, full-set-gated** is literally Alex's "gate the how" exception.
- **Number-drops and third-person receipts** ("this makes people $X") fit Alex's value-first, spoken-to-"you" style.

### Conflicts with Alex's HARD RULES + exact resolution
1. **No em-dashes** — Sabrina's ASR has stray dashes; strip them, use commas/periods. Trivial.
2. **No first-person anecdote (HER BIGGEST CONFLICT).** Sabrina is heavily first-person: "I tried this and it actually wiped my identity," "I sold my AI company," "I built Blotato." **Resolution:** (a) Convert the receipt "I tried this and it…" → third-person tension line: "this wiped someone's identity off hundreds of sites" / "this is finding money leaks owners miss for months." (b) Drop the "I sold my AI company at 30" credential entirely, or convert to a general authority beat ("this is the stuff operators quietly use"). (c) "I built Blotato" → name the artifact and sell the result, no ownership claim.
3. **Gate the how** — Sabrina already gates the *full* prompt set behind the keyword while showing rough steps. Keep her exact move: name the artifact, walk 2–3 rough steps, gate the complete prompts/schedule behind the keyword.
4. **Cut on the keyword** — Sabrina's tail "and I'll DM you" must be trimmed. End hard on "Comment [KEYWORD]."
5. **Value noun by ~word 12, no jargon, words ≤ sec×4.3** — her hooks already land the value noun by word 10; keep vocabulary at her plain-English level ("agents," "audit," "money leaks," never "orchestration layer").
6. **Never fabricate** — do not reuse her personal claims ("I sold my company," "I met a mom who…") as if Alex's. Keep authority beats true and general; if citing a receipt, keep it real and third-person.
7. **Breadth litmus** — Sabrina is natively broad (business owners, creators, non-technical). Keep the buyer generic ("any business owner / any creator") so a designer, a Shopify owner, a coach, and a founder all see themselves.

### Gate-the-how mapping (this creator's format)
- **Shown on screen:** the artifact name, the rough 2–3 spoken steps, one live result ("it drops the report in Slack before you wake up"), the outcome number.
- **Gated behind keyword:** the full word-for-word prompts, the exact schedule/config, the complete multi-agent setup.
