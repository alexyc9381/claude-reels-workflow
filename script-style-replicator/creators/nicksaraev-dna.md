# nicksaraev — Script DNA
> Source: 7 top-outlier YouTube transcripts (162K–314K views), fetched 2026-07-12. Weighted toward the two 250K+ website videos and the "just dropped" news-peg cluster. Reconciles + upgrades `memory/nick-saraev-style-reference.md`.

> **Upgrade note vs. prior ref:** the old ref pinned Nick's hook as "$ dollar result + I built it with Claude + here's the exact system." The transcripts SHARPEN this. On his biggest videos the dominant opener is a **news-peg + skeptic-deflate** ("Anthropic just launched X" / "everybody's saying X, here's the truth"), not the raw dollar-result line. The dollar receipt is real but it lives in the *body* as proof, not in the hook (only 1 of 7 opens on a build/result; 4 of 7 open on a "just [dropped/launched/released/built]" news event). The transferable engine is **"big new thing everyone is overcomplicating → here's how simple it actually is → receipts → scale it."**

## 0. Voiceprint
A former agency guy who's mildly annoyed that AI just commoditized his old job, narrating a live build in plain talk: news-peg the drop, call the hype BS, deflate the complexity ("it's literally just a prompt"), drop hard numbers as receipts, then dare you to go cash in.

## 1. Opener / Hook Formula

### Primary template (news-peg + implication), 4 of 7 videos
```
[Company/Person/repo] just [dropped | launched | released | built] [VALUE NOUN],
which [one-line of what it does / why it matters].
```
- "Well, **Anthropic just launched routines**, which allows Claude to kick off automations via schedule, trigger, or even webhook." (Routines, 210K)
- "**Anthropic just released manage agents**, which is their take on automating the process of automating processes." (Managed Agents, 176K)
- "**An open-source project just dropped** that, when you combine it with Claude code, literally becomes self-improving AI." (Karpathy Autoresearch, 168K)
- "Hey, **I just built these four websites** in about 15 minutes or so... because they all have really cool 3D sexy scroll effects." (Nano Banana/Kling, 314K)

The word **"just"** carries the freshness/urgency in the opener of 4 of 7. The value noun lands by ~word 5 in 5 of 7 openers (websites / routines / manage agents / self-improving AI / skills).

### Variant A — Contrarian / skeptic-deflate (2 of 7)
```
[Everybody] is [hyping / talking about] [THING].
I thought it was [hype BS] ... [and here's what's actually true].
```
- "So, with **everybody and their mom** talking about this giant red lobster, I thought that I'd give it a whirl and see for myself why Claudebot is supposedly the most powerful AI tool anybody has ever seen." → payoff: "**Claudebot sucks, actually. And here's why.**" (Clawdbot, 194K)
- "Hey, so Gemini 3.1's like really good at making websites. And **I initially thought that this was just going to be some hype BS**... but you know what I did? I copied his prompt... and it's really clean." (Gemini/Antigravity, 254K)

### Variant B — Relatable confession + tension (1 of 7)
```
I [freaking love / do a lot of] [THING]. I think you do too.
But [the flaw that this video fixes].
```
- "**I freaking love Claude code skills. I think you do too**, but sometimes they're a little bit unreliable. ... 70% of the time I get an intended output, but 30% of the time it's a bag of rocks." (Autoresearch Skills, 162K)

### The 4 rules that make the hook fire
1. **Peg to something that "just" happened.** Freshness is the click. If there's no launch, manufacture recency from a build you "just" did ("I just built these four websites in about 15 minutes").
2. **Undercut the hype in the same breath.** Skepticism = credibility. "I initially thought this was hype BS" / "anytime somebody tells me they oneshot stuff, I'm inherently skeptical." He earns trust by being the guy who calls BS, then shows it's real (or isn't).
3. **Name the value noun instantly, no throat-clearing.** websites, routines, agents, skills, self-improving AI — concrete deliverable in the first breath.
4. **Plant a stake / promise fast.** "There's really just three steps and I'm going to show them all to you right now." / "here's why." The hook ends pointing at a payoff.

## 2. Structural Skeleton (the beat map)

Nick does NOT use crisp punchy section labels (no RayFu-style "The Scanner. The Surgeon."). His grammar is a **conversational build-log**: peg → deflate → prove → scale → dare. But the beat order is highly consistent across all 7.

| # | Beat | Function | Verbatim example |
|---|------|----------|------------------|
| 1 | **The Drop** | News-peg or result-drop; name the value noun | "Anthropic just released manage agents, which is their take on automating the process of automating processes." |
| 2 | **The Skeptic Frame** | Undercut hype / state the flaw / set stakes | "anytime somebody tells me they oneshot stuff, I'm just like really inherently skeptical." |
| 3 | **The Credential/Receipt** | Establish authority + a hard number | "A few years back, this would probably be 5 to 10,000 dollars a pop and I know that because I used to sell websites for a living." |
| 4 | **The Deflate** | "Everyone overcomplicates this; it's actually just [N] steps / one prompt" | "it's way simpler than everybody else is making it out to be. You don't have to hop around like 30 different platforms. There's really just three steps." |
| 5 | **The Live Demo / Proof** | Walk the build, narrate reactions, drop token/time/$ costs | "that's what, three or four bucks, realistically?" / "it just compressed it from 5.3 megs down to 252 kilobytes." |
| 6 | **The Scale-Out** | "Now imagine this at 20x / running every night / across 20 instances" — the vision | "You could parallelize this, run this across five or 10 or 20 instances of Gemini Pro 3.1 and then you could send custom websites to whoever the hell you want." |
| 7 | **The CTA** | Free prompt/repo below + subscribe guilt-trip | "you can also get all the prompts... down below in the description. I'm giving all that stuff away for free... Something like 73% of viewers are not subscribed." |

**Target length / budget:** these outliers are 9–25 min long-form (1,833–5,575 words). For an **Alex reel (~35–45s, ~150–190 words)** compress to: Beat 1 (Drop) + Beat 2 (Skeptic/Deflate, merged) + Beat 3 (one Receipt) + Beat 5 (2–3 proof lines) + Beat 6 (one Scale line) + Beat 7 (gated CTA). Drop the meandering demo.

## 3. Per-Beat Micro-Templates

- **Drop:** `[Anthropic / a 16-year-old / an open-source repo] just [dropped/launched] [VALUE NOUN], which [does OUTCOME].`

## 4. Sentence Rhythm & Mechanics

- **Bimodal sentence length.** Long conversational explainers of **25–45 words** ("This environment can connect to mcp.clickup.com, it has no packages, it has MCP access enabled and its type is highly limited...") alternating with **2–6-word reaction fragments** ("This is cool." / "Look at that." / "Pretty clean. Pretty clean." / "It's not."). Blended average lands ~**18–22 words/sentence**, but the *texture* is the swing between the two.
- **Opener length:** first sentence averages ~**19 words** (range 6–40; median ~16). Short punchy openers (v7: "I freaking love Claude code skills." = 6 words) or a long single breath (v1: 30 words).
- **Imperative density: LOW in the hook, HIGH in demo + CTA.** Hooks are declarative narration ("Anthropic just released..."). Imperatives cluster where he pastes prompts ("Generate me a high-quality 3D render... Make the background white... make it faster... mobile optimize the site") and in CTA ("Find the prompt down below." / "click the subscribe button"). For a tight reel, keep 2–3 imperatives max, in the CTA.
- **Number/$ density: VERY HIGH — his signature.** Nearly every claim carries a hard figure: "$15K", "5 to 10,000 dollars a pop", "36 cents", "$5 in tokens", "5.3 megs down to 252 kilobytes", "73%", "81.3% improvement", "1,100 milliseconds down to 67", "2 cents per generation", "20 cents per test", "$204 in total". Rule: **every proof beat gets a specific number, and specific beats vague** (never "cheap," always "36 cents").
- **Causal connectors dominate.** Heavy **"because" / "so" / "and so" / "the reason why" / "which means"**. "The reason I want to make this video is because..." / "And so the reason why I'm equating it like this is because routines effectively solve that middle problem." He constantly explains *why*.
- **Sentence openers:** disproportionately "So," "Now," "Okay," "Anyway," "And so," "Um." "Anyway" is his **reset button** (returns from a tangent). "To make a long story short" (3 of 7) and "in a nutshell" precede a compression.
- **Filler to STRIP for Alex:** raw ASR is dense with "like," "you know," "sort of," "kind of," "um," "and so on and so forth," "whatever the heck." These are the spoken-YouTube texture; the written reel keeps the *cadence* (swing + causal "because") but cuts the filler.
- **Signature punctuation/cadence:** the **doubled short beat** — "Pretty clean. Pretty clean." / "That is clean. That is really clean." / "Yeah. Yeah." — repeat-for-emphasis. And the **rule of three** everywhere (three steps / three ingredients / three simple steps).

## 6. Retention / Escalation Devices

- **Live reaction beats** re-hook mid-demo: "[gasps]," "Look at that," "That is clean. That is really clean." He performs surprise so you feel it too.
- **The running cost meter.** He constantly re-drops the price as it accrues ("$5 in tokens," "another dollar or so," "36 cents," "that's what, three or four bucks") — a continuity thread that keeps "this is cheap" alive.
- **"Here's the trick / here's why" pivots.** "And here's why." / "there's kind of a clever reason for this that I'll get into." Open-loop, delivered-later.
- **Complexity-then-collapse.** He shows a scary wall (a 17-step framework, drag-and-drop n8n spaghetti, "35,000 steps") then collapses it to "one prompt." The *contrast* is the retention.
- **The Scale-Out reveal (the "now imagine" beat).** After proving one instance, he 10x's the vision: "run this across 20 instances... spin up dozens of products a day." Re-hooks by expanding the stakes.
- **Number-drops as mini-payoffs.** Every "1,100 milliseconds down to 67" / "81.3% improvement" is a dopamine hit that resets attention.
- **Meta/aside humor.** "insert current year here... I was told not to discuss the current year by a YouTube consultant." / "I guess I already just leaked my API key, whatever the hell." Keeps it human and unpredictable.

## 7. Proof & Credibility Style

- **Former-agency credential** (2 of 7, load-bearing): "I used to sell websites for a living" / "as a former website designer, I'm kind of pissed cuz that's like my whole business commoditized." Authority = "I did the expensive old way, so I know what it used to cost."
- **Real dollar receipts, third-person and first-person:** "5 to 10,000 dollars a pop" (old cost), "$50,000 plus month B2B agencies," "$1M/y," "$204 in total last month," "$2.40 today."
- **Live, on-screen metrics:** cold-email reply rates ("2.4%," "1.5% → 2.7%"), load time ("1,100ms → 67ms," "81.3%"), token counts ("22.3 million tokens in"), file sizes ("5.3 megs → 252 kilobytes"), eval scores ("32/40 → 39/40").
- **Authority name-drops (real, verifiable — never fabricated quotes):** Andrej Karpathy ("former founding member of OpenAI, later head of AI at Tesla"), Peter Steinberger (Clawdbot dev), "a really cracked 16-year-old" / "Leon's skill" on Twitter, Brian Johnson (a DMT joke). Tools: Claude Code, Antigravity, Kling 3.0, Higgsfield, Nano Banana, Netlify, Vercel, n8n, make.com, Zapier, ClickUp, Instantly, Fireflies, WhisperFlow, GitHub Actions.
- **He debunks with proof too** (Clawdbot): cites the pump-and-dump timeline, "$16 mil... 90% token crash... $300 spent in two days," "over 900 Cloudbot instances with no security." His skepticism is evidenced, not vibes.

## 9. Why It Works (transferable mechanism)

- **Freshness is the click.** "Just dropped" pegs the video to a live wave; viewers feel they're catching something before everyone else.
- **Skepticism buys trust.** By being the guy who calls hype BS, he becomes the credible narrator — so when he says "this one's real," you believe it.
- **Complexity-collapse is deeply satisfying.** Showing the scary 17-step version and then "it's literally one prompt" makes the viewer feel let in on a shortcut the gurus are hiding.
- **Specific numbers beat adjectives.** "36 cents," "1,100ms → 67ms," "$204" — concreteness reads as honesty and makes the outcome feel real and repeatable.
- **The Scale-Out sells the dream without lying.** He proves ONE small case, then says "now imagine this 20x," letting the viewer extrapolate the money themselves.

## 10. Adapting to Alex (the fusion layer)

### Transfers cleanly to faceless mascot
- **The news-peg opener** ("Claude just shipped X / a free repo just dropped") — perfect for a clay-mascot cold-open; no face needed.
- **Complexity-collapse** ("everyone's overcomplicating this; it's one prompt / three agents") — a screen-recording of a single clean run IS the payoff.
- **Number-drop receipts** — burn-in captions love "36 cents," "39/40," "30% more per customer."
- **The Scale-Out "now imagine" line** — one sentence of aspiration, mascot gesturing wide.
- **Reaction beats** ("look at that," "that's clean") map to mascot reaction cuts.

### Conflicts with Alex's HARD RULES + exact resolution
| Nick's habit | Alex rule | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavy first-person anecdote** ("I built these," "I used to sell websites for a living," "I do a lot of cold email") | **No first-person anecdote** | Convert to **third-person receipt** or a "you" tension line. "I used to sell websites for $10K" → "This used to cost $10K a pop." "I built these four sites" → "Someone just built four of these in 15 minutes." Authority stays via the general truth ("web design used to cost thousands"), never an invented personal story. |
| **Gives the full prompt/repo away free** ("no gatekeeping," "prompts down below") | **Gate the how** | Keep the *result* and the *artifact name* in the VO; move the full prompt/skill/repo behind the **comment keyword**. "Everything's free below" → "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the exact prompt." Partial spoken steps ("it's three agents: a CFO, a customer, a referee") are fine as value; the word-for-word system is gated. |
| **Trailing tail after the ask** ("...and yeah, I'll catch y'all in the next video") | **Cut on the keyword** | End hard on the keyword. Trim every "have a lovely rest of your day" tail. Last word = the keyword. |
| **Occasional jargon** (MCP, webhook, orchestrator, hyperparameters, validation loss) | **Zero jargon, 12-yo parse** | Swap for plain outcomes: "orchestrator" → "the boss agent," "webhook trigger" → "it fires by itself," "validation loss" → "how good it is." Keep the deliverable noun concrete. |
| **Meanders / long demo** | **~35–45s, value noun by word 12** | Compress to Drop → Deflate → 1 Receipt → 2 proof lines → 1 Scale line → gated CTA. Value noun in the first breath. |
| **Mild profanity / "whatever the hell"** | (tone) | Drop the profanity; keep the casual "here's the thing" register. |
| Could imply a **run he didn't do** | **Never fabricate** | Keep receipts true and general ("this kind of setup costs pennies to run"); don't attach a fake specific number to a run that didn't happen or a quote to a named person. |

### Gate-the-how mapping (Nick format → Alex)
- **Shown in VO (free):** the news peg, the outcome, the artifact's *name and shape* ("a council of three Claude agents: a CFO, a customer, a referee"), the rough cost/result, one proof number.
- **Gated behind keyword:** the exact prompt text, the full role definitions, the loop/schedule config, the repo link.

## 12. Worked Example (fresh Alex-niche topic, run through the HARD RULES)

**Topic:** An "AI council" — three Claude agents that argue over your pricing and ship the winner. **Keyword: COUNCIL.**

> A founder just let three Claude agents argue about their pricing for an hour. The winner made them thirty percent more per customer, and they never opened a spreadsheet.
>
> Here's the thing everyone overcomplicates. You don't need a pricing consultant. You don't need a course. You need a council.
>
> It's three agents. One plays the greedy CFO who wants to charge more. One plays the nervous customer who's about to walk. One plays the referee. You hand them your current offer, they fight it out, and the referee ships the version that wins.
>
> The wild part: it costs about forty cents to run, and you can loop it every night while you sleep. So it keeps testing prices without you.
>
> Most people are going to turn this into a twelve-step framework. It's not. It's one prompt and three roles.
>
> Want the exact council prompt, the three roles, and the loop that keeps only the winner? Comment COUNCIL.

**Rule check:** ~160 words (~37s). No em-dashes. No first-person anecdote (opens third-person "A founder just..."). Value noun ("pricing") by word 9. News-peg + deflate + receipt (30% / 40 cents) + scale ("loop it every night") + gated CTA cut hard on **COUNCIL**. Jargon-free (12-yo parse). Keeps Nick's DNA: "just," "here's the thing everyone overcomplicates," rule of three, "the wild part," doubled short beats ("It's not."), hard numbers.
