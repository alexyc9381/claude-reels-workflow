If you take notes, Claude just became your best thinking partner. But first, you need to download this free note-taking tool called Obsidian. It links all your notes as .md files in one place called a vault. Say your project notes connect to your meeting notes, or your ideas connect to your goals. Next, simply connect this to Claude code using the Obsidian CLI, and it will build an entire web of how you actually think. After that, ask Claude to build you custom commands. That falls into two categories: daily operations, like a today command, that pulls your calendar, tasks, and recent notes into a one-day prioritized plan. Then, there's thinking commands. Say an emerge command that surfaces ideas you've been circling for months, but never actually named. And that's how Claude connects the dots for you.
