## THE ARC (must be one story, not 8 slides)
**Setup** (S1–S2): your bill is wrong, and a dead poet is the reason → **Escalating reveals** (S3–S5): three
poets, each bigger, each a size → **PEAK / payoff** (S6): the names are a spec sheet, you're paying symphony
money for haiku work → **Twist/resolution** (S7): there's a newest branch, and the tortoise that's been
crawling through the whole reel finally arrives with him → **CTA** (S8).
Intensity curve: 6 → 4 → 5 → 6.5 → 8 → **10** → 7.5 → 8.5. Rises to the peak at S6. No mid sag.

## ⭐ THE RUNNING GAG (threaded S1→S7, pays off)
**A tortoise crawls slowly across the bottom of the panel in EVERY scene**, a few pixels further each time,
ignored by everyone. In **S7 it finally arrives** — because it's **Aesop's tortoise**, and Aesop is Fable,
the newest branch. The slowest thing on screen wins the race to the punchline. Nobody gets it until S7;
everybody re-watches S1 to check it was always there. *(This is the loop-back re-watch driver.)*

## ⭐ THE POP-CULTURE CAST (cast at storyboard time, per the standing rule)
**Knockoff Ninja Turtles** ("RENAISSANCE BOYS") — because they are *literally named after dead Renaissance
artists*, which is the exact joke Anthropic is making. They appear as bystanders who **get it before the
viewer does**: a knockoff Donatello nods sagely at the portrait wall (S2), a knockoff Raphael elbows him at
S6's reveal. This is the only cameo family — it reinforces the premise instead of decorating it.
**Living world:** candle flicker + quill scratch (S4), metronome tick (S5), sheet music fluttering, dust motes.

## NUMBER SPINE (identical everywhere — never drifts)
**1694** · **17** · **154** · **125**. ⛔ **No invented dollar figure anywhere.** The price comparison is a
RELATIVE scale (a bar/ladder), captured from the real pricing page (see capture contract).

# CARDS

## S1 — HOOK (0.0–4.5s) · intensity 6
1. **LINE:** "A Japanese poet who died in 1694 is why your Claude bill is too high."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** *Your bill is wrong, and the reason is absurdly old.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** A dark panel. A **towering stack of glowing invoices**. Standing on top, absurdly
   small, a **straw-hatted poet sprite (BASHŌ)** calmly holding a brush. "**1694**" slams in, huge, chiseled.
   **WHY:** the collision *ancient poet ↔ modern bill* IS the hook, so frame 0 must show both objects
   touching. Mute-legible in <2s: big number + a bill + a man who does not belong there.
   **REJECTED:** a model-picker dropdown opening (accurate but it's UI, not an image — zero stopping power
   at frame 0, and it leaks the answer). **REJECTED:** a burning bill (generic; every finance reel does it).
4. **PROOF:** the invoice stack is real Claude billing chrome, blurred; the **only** crisp thing is "1694".
5. **ESCALATION:** n/a (opening) — but frame 0 must MOVE: the invoice stack is still settling/teetering.
6. **GAG:** Bashō is unbothered, balancing on a teetering stack. **Tortoise enters frame bottom-left (1/8).**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** hard cut in. Stone-slam on "1694" + a paper-flutter bed.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ big date + bills + tiny poet = "something old is costing me money."

## S2 — TURN (4.5–10.0s) · intensity 4 (deliberate breath before the climb)
1. **LINE:** "Every Claude model is named after a kind of poem, and the poem is a size."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** *The names aren't names. They're a system.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** The invoice stack **dissolves into a portrait wall** — a dynastic gallery, four gilt
   frames, three lit and **one dark/empty** (that's Fable, S7's setup). A ribbon reads **HAIKU · SONNET ·
   OPUS · ?**. **WHY:** a portrait wall is the family-tree artifact *in embryo* — the reel's payoff object
   introduced early and empty, so the viewer wants it filled. The empty 4th frame IS the open loop.
   **REJECTED:** an actual branching tree diagram this early — it gives away S8's artifact and flattens the
   build. Save the tree.
4. **PROOF:** the ribbon labels are the real model names in Claude's actual type.
5. **ESCALATION:** scope widens — one bill → a whole dynasty. Camera pulls back (new motion type).
6. **GAG:** a **knockoff Donatello** stands in the gallery nodding sagely, hands behind shell — *he knows
   about being named after a dead artist.* **Tortoise 2/8.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** invoice-to-portrait particle dissolve. Low choral swell + a gallery hush.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ four frames, one empty = "there's a family, and one's missing."

## S3 — HAIKU (10.0–16.0s) · intensity 5
1. **LINE:** "Haiku is seventeen syllables. Bashō wrote them in one breath. Tiny, fast, and it costs almost nothing."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** *Haiku = small and cheap.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** BASHŌ frame lights. He exhales **ONE breath** that becomes **17 glowing syllable-dots**
   that snap into a tiny poem, then into a tiny **Claude reply bubble**. A feather-light **cost pip** barely
   tips a scale. **WHY:** "one breath" is literal and mute-legible; counting dots gives the eye the number 17
   without reading. Distinct base object = **breath/dots**.
   **REJECTED:** a stopwatch (speed ≠ size, and it duplicates S5's metronome).
4. **PROOF:** a real Claude Haiku reply, short, timestamped fast. On-screen number: **17**.
5. **ESCALATION:** first frame lights — the wall starts filling. Motion: exhale/particle.
6. **GAG:** Bashō finishes the poem, and the poem is just "**bill: too big**" — a 17-syllable complaint.
   **Tortoise 3/8.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** whip-pan right along the wall. Soft koto pluck + a single breath.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ 17 dots → tiny bubble → scale barely moves.

## S4 — SONNET (16.0–22.0s) · intensity 6.5
1. **LINE:** "Sonnet is fourteen lines. Shakespeare wrote a hundred and fifty-four of them. That's the everyday workhorse."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** *Sonnet = the balanced default you should mostly live on.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** SHAKESPEARE frame lights; a **ruffed sprite at a writing desk** stamps out sonnets on
   a **conveyor belt**, a counter racing to **154**. Each sonnet = 14 ruled lines. **WHY:** the conveyor
   makes "workhorse" an image, not an adjective, and a racing counter is escalation you can *see*. Distinct
   base object = **conveyor/desk**.
   **REJECTED:** a quill writing in cursive (pretty, but static and unreadable on mute).
4. **PROOF:** real Claude Sonnet reply doing an everyday task. On-screen numbers: **14**, **154**.
5. **ESCALATION:** counter to 154 + the scale now visibly dips. Bigger, louder, faster than S3.
6. **GAG:** the belt jams at sonnet **#18** and Shakespeare kicks it (it's the famous one, *"Shall I compare
   thee…"*). **Tortoise 4/8, now crawling ON the conveyor, going backwards.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** belt-clank into a whip-pan. Quill scratch + ratcheting counter clicks.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ conveyor + counter hitting 154 = "this one does the volume."

## S5 — OPUS (22.0–29.0s) · intensity 8
1. **LINE:** "Opus is just Latin for the work. Beethoven's ninth symphony is Opus one twenty-five. That's the masterwork, and it's priced like one."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** *Opus = the huge expensive one.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** OPUS frame **bursts** its gilt edge. **BEETHOVEN sprite** (wild hair) conducts a full

   realisation and not a bigger spectacle. Distinct base object = **orchestra/podium**.
   **REJECTED:** a bigger stack of money (duplicates S1's bills — the "no repeated base metaphor" rule).
4. **PROOF:** the real pricing scale, Opus vs Haiku, as a RELATIVE ladder. On-screen number: **125**.
   ⛔ **No invented dollar figure** — capture from the real pricing page.
5. **ESCALATION:** peak spectacle. Orchestra + panel crack. Loudest beat so far.
6. **GAG:** the whole orchestra plays a **thunderous fanfare**, and the on-screen Claude task it's answering
   is "**what's 2+2**". The absurdity IS the argument. **Tortoise 5/8, wearing tiny ear defenders.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** orchestral hit → sudden dead silence (the S6 setup). Timpani + a panel-crack crunch.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ giant orchestra answering "2+2" + scale slamming = "overkill, and it costs."

## S6 — L4 PAYOFF (29.0–34.0s) · intensity 10 ⭐ THE PEAK
1. **LINE:** "So the names were never decoration. They're a spec sheet. And if you run everything on Opus, you're paying symphony money to write a haiku."
2. **THE ONE TAKEAWAY:** ⭐ *The name IS the spec sheet. You've been reading a price tag as a brand name.*
3. **STORY metaphor:** **Dead silence.** The three portraits **rotate in unison** and their backs are a
   **spec sheet / price ladder** — the gilt frames were labels all along. Then the killer image: the **entire
   orchestra crammed into the tiny 17-dot haiku bubble**, playing a fanfare for one line. **WHY:** the
   payoff must be a *re-frame of something already on screen*, not new information — so we literally FLIP
   the objects the viewer has been watching for 25s. The silence + rotate is the cheapest, hardest-hitting
   pattern interrupt available and it lands on the reel's one non-obvious idea.
   **REJECTED:** a talking-head-style text card stating the lesson (it's a proposition, not an object —
   the exact thing [[creator-lane-ceilings]]'s discriminator says loses).
4. **PROOF:** the flipped frames show the real relative price ladder. Numbers: **17 / 14 / 125** aligned.
5. **ESCALATION:** inverts — silence after the loudest beat. Loudest → quietest is the biggest step of all.
6. **GAG:** **knockoff Raphael elbows knockoff Donatello** — *they've known about dead-artist names since 1984.*
   **Tortoise 6/8, dead centre, finally noticeable.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** ⛔ **full stop.** All beds cut. One vinyl-scratch, then a single low sub-boom on
   "spec sheet."
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ frames flip to price tags + orchestra squeezed in a tiny bubble = the whole thesis, wordless.

## S7 — FABLE / THE TWIST (34.0–37.0s) · intensity 7.5

6. **GAG:** ⭐ **the tortoise's payoff** — the slowest thing on screen has been walking to its own reveal since
   second one. Aesop pats it. (*Tortoise and the Hare.*) **Tortoise 7/8 — arrived.**
7. **TRANSITION + SFX:** frame-ignite whoosh + a warm chord resolving. Tiny triumphant tortoise sting.
8. **MUTE CHECK:** ✅ 4th frame lights + tree assembles = "the family is complete."
